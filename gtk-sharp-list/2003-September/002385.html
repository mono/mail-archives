On Fri, 2003-08-29 at 13:29, Todd Berman wrote:

> With gtk-sharp starting to include more and more, wnck, gnome-print,
> etc, it seems to me that it might make sense to split the glue up, so
> that instead of glue being a toplevel folder, it is actually underneath
> gtk, glib, etc.

I think it's better to have all the glue code in one place, but there
have been other people suggest breaking it up into more focused glue
libs in the past.  I would probably accept a patch to split it into
libgtksharpglue and libgnomesharpglue.  I don't see a lot of point to
fragmenting it beyond that.

> I think that would help with not only packaging the library, but also
> the importation of other Gobject based wrappers into the gtk-sharp
> distribution (diacanvas-sharp comes to mind).

Gtk# will only be including gnome platform libraries.  I don't see how
fragmenting the glue really helps to include platform libraries, but I
agree that it may help clarify packaging issues.
