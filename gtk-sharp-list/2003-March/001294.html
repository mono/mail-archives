Hi! I was looking today at the gtk-sharp/gnometutorial/PEOPLE and
acknowledged that I should have contributed much more than I did (only
the introduction to Eclipse :(), it's only that I got distracted by
trivial things like trying to get a job to pay my education. 

I just wanted to tell you that the Mencoder front-end for ripping DVDs
that is mentioned in gtk-sharp/gnometutorial/PEOPLE is in
http://gnomencoder.sourceforge.net, I just thought that it could be used
for more than just eating dust in the corner, i.e. as an example
application for beginners. It's a hack, I know, but if more people put
their eyes into it I can improve it so that newcomers can understand it
better. In this little app I coped with issues like interacting with
native libraries, launching processes, use of threads, using gconf, made
a clear distinction on base classes, interfaces and implementations
(obvious OO stuff, but maybe not for newbies), among others.

You just tell me if it can serve for anything. Maybe just making a list
of little apps for beginners would help.
