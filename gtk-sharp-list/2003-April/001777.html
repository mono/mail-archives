Speaking of integrating with .NET.  GnomeVFS# totally integrates with
the .NET System.IO stuff.

Once we can specify (in the .metadata files) that a (generated) class
implements an interface; and that a (generated) method needs the
"override" keyword added, then we can integrate GDA# (from GnomeDB#)
with ADO.NET.

And, we could even do things to integrate Gtk# with 
System.Windows.Forms.  (Like making Gtk# widgets implement
the proper stuff to make them so you can use them in VS.NET's
GUI builder.  Or you could just do this with a wrapper class.)
