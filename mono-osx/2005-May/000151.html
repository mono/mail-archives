On 15-May-05, at 12:42 PM, Oscar Papel wrote:

> I'm guessing that you have a very old version of mono located in  
> your /usr/bin or /usr/local/bin subdirectories.  Also, if you have  
> used a tool like fink or darwinports in the past, check your /opt/ 
> bin or /sw/bin subdirectories.  The Mono installer post-install  
> script should have made symlinks in your /usr/bin sub that point  
> to /Library/Frameworks/Mono.framework.  It may have failed, though,  
> if you already had mono installed "manually" with another tool.
>
> Regardless, check your PATH to see what directories are being  
> searched and verify that there are no old copies of mono in any of  
> them.
>
> Oscar
