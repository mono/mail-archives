On 22.04.2010 19:57, Dirk Roeleveld wrote:
> So what does this tell us?
>
> Should I be referencing it by putting in
> -r:$prefix/lib/mono/2.0/System.Web.Extensions.dll in the command line for
> gmcs? Or does gmcs pay attention to web.config and I should be mentioning
> System.Web.Extensions.dll inside of web.config?

gmcs-r:System.Web.Extensions.dll *must* work if your Mono is correctly
installed and if the assembly exists and it's accessible.

Check the output of

	cat `type -p gmcs`

on your system. It must be something like that:

	mono $prefix/lib/mono/2.0/gmcs.exe

Does gmcs.exe's path match your $prefix?

On web.config:

Gmcs does not look at it if you compile assemblies manually.

OTOH, compilations triggered by ASP.NET are heavily configured
with a web.config. See the attached generic (boilerplate)
web.config which enables ASP.NET 3.5 for a web application.

>
> Or is this some other way to tell gmcs to look in the GAC?

No. Compilers never look in the GAC because the GAC may
contain several versions of the same assembly.
