we will generated them from the original headers.  Writing Cocoa apps will
require a lot of Cocoa specific knowledge and even some knowledge about
Objective-C messaging runtime behavior to understand how things fit
together, Objective-C is not strictly typed and otherwise does not fix
perfectly into the .Net universe.  Having different naming schemes will be
the least of evils in my opinion, and having the method names not match up
the Cocoa documentation will not help understand that complex framework.
But hey what do I know, I just wrote a two way bridge for that.
