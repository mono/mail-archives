Hi Tim,

> I was talking to kangaroo and he didn't think that the old  way of 
> using the tableview was the proper way, which is why it's not  working 
> now.  The old way consisted of setting the DataSource object  as the 
> datasource for the tableview in the nib file.
>
> Then inside your app you would have to call mytableview.target=this.
> Then you could add data to the datasource and call  
> mytableview.reloadData
>
> The tableview would work fine.
>
> So obviously this IS the wrong way, lol.  Just for the record  
> Andreas, what is the proper way of doing this?

The way I understood to do it is to instanciate TableView (adding 
columns etc.), then set the DataSource property and do a ReloadData ... 
this way it has worked for me. I didn't use target for this before, and 
kangaroo and me agreed on not exposing target, action and other 
selector-related messages directly in Cocoa#.

I didn't try using TableView from a nib but it does not depend on any 
managed instance variables so it should work just the same.
