Actually, I think I'm wrong, now that i think about it some more.  The @"" 
means something totally different in C# then it means in ObjC.

In ObjC, @"foo" means [[NSString alloc] initWithCString:"foo"]

In C#, @"foo" means dont interpret \ characters as "C" escape characters
so "foo\\bar" and @"foo\bar" are identical.

In your case, @"Add" is equivalent to "Add".  The @"" in C# is useful mostly 
for harcoding file paths into C# code, and then only PC paths since other 
OS's use "foo/bar" instead.

I find when I'm having trouble with CocoaSharp code, the best thing to do is 
to rewrite a test case in ObjC.  If it works there then you have discovered 
a bug and should report it.  I think you may have discovered a bug.
