I was about to file a bug with Mono, you having said that. Then I
noticed that you are not just using DateTime.Now like you said but
instead using it with a *custom* format string in a Console.WriteLine
operation (src/Cocoa/Mach.cs). It appears the month and minutes are
swapped, and the hours do not follow the local setting of 24-hour format.

There would be several patch possibilities in this case:
a) Simply swap MM and mm. This would not change 12-hour to 24-hour format.
b) The above plus change hour to 24-hour format by default. Would change
it to 24-hour format also for users used to a 12-hour format.
c) Remove the custom format and use Mono's standard DateTime.ToString
globalized output.
