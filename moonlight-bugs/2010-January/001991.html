Our current implementation of Popup propagates the 'IsEnabled' state from its
parent to its 'Child'. This was done to allow one of the DRTs to pass. I've
written up about a dozen new moon-unit tests and it looks like the behaviour is
quite simple (I haven't checked if this changed between SL2 and SL3 yet
though).

Currently the behaviour is this:
1) Popup does not propagate IsEnabled to its subtree
2) ComboBox does some magic to propagate IsEnabled to all its containers, and
just its containers, when its Enabled state is changed.

To implement this I could add a p/invokable method:
Control::UpdateParentEnabled (bool). ComboBox could then invoke this method on
all its containers whenever its enabled state changes. The question is, is this
the right thing to do? It feels like yet another horrible hack but I'm not sure
how else it's supposed to work.
