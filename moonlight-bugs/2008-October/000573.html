--- Comment #3 from Chris Toshok <toshok at novell.com>  2008-10-13 12:32:43 MDT ---
yeah, the trouble is that they hide the cursor by setting it to "None".  We
can't deal with that in a windowless context, so you get whatever cursor
mozilla last changed the cursor to -- if you were over a textfield, you'll get
an I-beam.  if you were over a link, you'd get a hand, etc, etc.

This is on top of the cursor they're using internally - they set the window
system cursor to None, then track the pointer location and draw their own with
xaml.  That's why you see two cursors.

So long story short, indeed it's a dup of 364990.  Marking as such.
