--- Comment #5 from Aaron Bockover <abockover at novell.com>  2009-03-26 15:17:21 MST ---
After further investigation, I cannot reproduce a crash. I can tell you that
the NPAPI support in WebKit/QT is incredibly broken and does not follow the
NPAPI specification though.

WebKit/QT is calling NP_Initialize on all plugins. The *ONLY* function that the
browser should call against a plugin is NP_GetMIMEDescription, UNLESS there is
content in the DOM that requests a plugin for a mimetype that exists in the
data returned by a plugin when the browser called NP_GetMIMEDescription.

In short, WebKit/QT initializes all plugins in the system even if they will
never be needed for a given page.

Additionally, Moonlight itself does not even load (something is going wrong
when resolving paths it seems) in WebKit/QT, and Moonshine (which in turn tries
to load Moonlight) fails to load it, but does not crash against WebKit/QT trunk
and Arora trunk.

I'm punting back to the Moonlight team for now. When Moonlight itself works in
WebKit/QT (not that it's the Moonlight team's fault it doesn't currently),
Moonshine bugs can be entertained against non-Mozilla-based browsers.

Also reducing severity and priority for a few reasons:

 * We do not ship a WebKit/QT on 11.1 that supports plugins
 * We do not ship Arora in 11.1
 * Moonlight has made no claims yet to work in non-Mozilla browsers
 * A user in 11.1 would never see this bug unless they managed to build both
WebKit/QT and Arora from trunk
