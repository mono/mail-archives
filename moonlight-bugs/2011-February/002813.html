Test Case

User-Agent:       Mozilla/5.0 (X11; U; Linux x86_64; en-US) AppleWebKit/534.10
(KHTML, like Gecko) Chrome/8.0.552.237 Safari/534.10

If I have a UserControl with its TabNavigation property set to cycle, when
pressing tab, focus will navigate through all its controls.

However, if one of those controls is itself another UserControl, focus should
begin to navigate within the controls of the inner usercontrol.

This is was happens in SL. In ML, however, the focus will stay only on the
controls of the outer usercontrol, not being passed on to the controls inside
the inner usercontrol until the user clicks one of them.

I am attaching a repro of this behaviour.

Reproducible: Always

Steps to Reproduce:
1. Run the TestPage.html of the attached test-case.
2. You will see three buttons over a white background. Those buttons are direct
children of the Page.xaml, the outer usercontrol. You will also see a few more
controls over a wheat background. The wheat background is the InnerView.xaml,
the inner usercontrol.
3. Click the first button, "A Button" to give it focus.
4. Keep on pressing TAB.
5. Notice that the focus only iterates through the 3 buttons in the outer
usercontrol and it never goes to the controls in the inner usercontrol.
6. Click one of the controls in the inner usercontrol, preferably the first
one.
7. Keep on pressing TAB.
8. Notice that eventually the focus is returned to the first control in the
outer usercontrol, which is fine, but that it never returns to the controls in
the inner usercontrol, which is not fine.
9. Try out the same experiment in SL.
10. See that the focus happily navigates between all the controls.
Actual Results:  
When pressing TAB key successively, focus is never passed to the controls in
the inner usercontrol.

Expected Results:  
When pressing TAB key successively, focus should eventually be passed to the
controls in the inner usercontrol.
