This is a really wierd bug but I've been able to reproduce it multiple times so
it seems legit. 

Basically what happens is when you have both Adobe-flash(My version was:
10.0.45.2) and the Moonlight-add-on wich up to date was version 2.2(the plugin
was versioned as: 3.0.40818.0 ) installed in firefox and you start your browser
and move to a site with flash-content the flash-clip will load but then it will
dissapear. No error message or anything.

Intresting enough is that if your browser has a start-page wich has
flash-content flash works as it should.

Workaround is to disable the moonlight-plugin reload the page with the
flash-content and then enable the plugin again. 

Reproducible: Always

Steps to Reproduce:
1. Have flash and moonlight installed in firefox. And both should be enabled.
2. Restart your browser so it's a fresh session where none of the pages have
flash-content. 
3. Go to a site with flash content like youtube.com and click on a clip there.
The clip will seem to load but will never actually show up.

4. Disable Moonlight and reload the page and the flash will now work. 
Enabling the Moonlight-plugin again and things will work properly until you
restart your browser like in step 2.
Actual Results:  
The flash seems to load but never shows up.

Expected Results:  
The flash-content should load as per flash-behaviour

since the workaround is to disable the moonlight-plugin reload the page with
the flash-content and then enable the moonlight-plugin again i felt it related
to something in the moonlight-plugin.
