I use it now, but I think "on by default" is a *very* bad idea. It 
causes far more frustration to have content there that you didn't type, 
than to type content that you know your code needs anyway.

It took me quite a while to get used to MonoDevelop's "auto-bracket" 
behaviour compared to Eclipse's, and I know there are others who would 
just hate it as a default.
