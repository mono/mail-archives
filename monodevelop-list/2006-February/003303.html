> The story is the following:
> 
> When the first Glade-3 development snapshot was released some months
> ago, it looked like a good chance to integrate a GUI designer in
> MonoDevelop. Glade-3 provided, for the first time, a set of widgets
> which could be easily integrated into an application like MD. Moreover,
> the development of Stetic (the GUI designer specially designed for Mono)
> was stopped since Dan (the maintainer) had to move to another Novell
> project. That's why the glade-3 integration work started.
> 
> However, after doing the main integration work, it became clear that
> Glade-3 fell a bit short of functionality (in its current development
> status) for what we want to achieve with MonoDevelop. So, given the fact
> that I'll have to spend time hacking on a GUI designer in order to match
> the needs of MD, I prefer hacking on Stetic rather than on Glade-3. So
> that's what I've been doing the last weeks. I plan to commit the new
> integrated designer next week.
> 
> To summarize: MonoDevelop will have its own GUI designer (based on
> Stetic). There won't be Glade-3 integration (unless somebody else wants
> to continue the work for some reason).
>
