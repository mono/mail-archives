On Wed, Feb 3, 2010 at 3:09 AM, Greg Bulmash <burgerguy at gmail.com> wrote:
>
> Yeah, but can you do it without the visual designer, specifying elements in
> code? Are there any tutorials on how to do that? Or are you telling Mac
> people that MonoDevelop for Mac is really just a big prank being played on
> Mac owners to frustrate us?

Yes, you can do GTK#, Winforms, ASP.NET (MVC), Moonlight, and console
development without a designer, or if you're using MonoTouch or a
third-party Cocoa wrapper you can also use Apple's Interface Builder.

For GTK# you may find these resources useful:
http://zetcode.com/tutorials/gtksharptutorial/
http://mjhutchinson.com/journal/2010/01/25/integrating_gtk_application_mac

> BTW, why isn't the list of disabled features on Mac easier to find? Figuring
> out which things aren't actually available on Mac seems like a treasure
> hunt. The GTK# designer doesn't work... oh and debugging doesn't either...

http://monodevelop.com/Download/What%27s_new_in_MonoDevelop_2.2#Feature_Matrix

The debugger works fine as long as you have Mono 2.6.x+ as your target runtime.
