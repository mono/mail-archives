Interestingly, there's nothing stopping XnaTouch or Mono.XNA from extending
XNA with shader model 4 features (e.g. through GLSL 1.3+). Obviously, you'll
need to provide multiple codepaths if you wish your game to run on the
original XNA framework, but that's no different from writing code specific
to the capabilities of the platform (you wouldn't use the same code on the
Zune as the XBox, for example).

XnaTouch (and Mono.XNA) can be 'yet another platform', but they can also be
much more.
