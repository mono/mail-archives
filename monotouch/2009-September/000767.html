Its just comes back down to basic market economics in the end, price
it high (i.e. Unity3D, Photoshop) and you'll get fewer devs with a
higher margin.
Price it at mainstream price (i.e. Resharper) you'll get a lot more
devs, which in turn will generate a community, market and mindshare
around the technology.

I think iPhone development is a phenomenon, generating more
ISV's/indie developers than anything else has in a very long time, so
I hope they capitalize on this
and choose a mainstream price model that provides good value for the
indie developer (i.e. similar to R#).

- Demis
