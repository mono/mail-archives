Yeah I don't think the marketing 'tag-line' of  'if you think it's not
worth it don't buy it, go use objective-c'
is going to fly in any competing marketing department, as most people
probably will - and Novell would've just lost a prospective customer
interested in their product.

The point is we all want to use this technology, we all want to see a
community and support around it and we all want to see it succeed.

Novell should be rewarded for its efforts, but the fact is that a
personal licence of $399 is going to price most indie / hobby
developers out - (which probably is the majority of all developers,
judging by the median quality of the apps in the AppStore). This in
turn means a smaller community and less supporting documentation,
blogs and tutorials as a result - something which no-one wants to see
happen.

Obviously Novell's product department has looked at the potential
market segment and have made projections on sales and prices based on
it in order to maximize their revenue, I just would've thought that
since most of iPhone developers are indie/hobbyists that a licence
tailored for them would make financial sense.

Maybe a Lite-version with no frills or support could be in the works?
