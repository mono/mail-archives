I think its fine to make a proprietary product, (which I will probably
be buying myself since it will be covered from App store revenue)

But you should consider using a different branding as the 'Mono' name
=~ 'OpenSource .NET' =~ 'Free .NET that runs on Free OS's'
So putting a proprietary product under the 'Mono'Touch name will most
likely cause confusion for a lot of people, who will probably by
extension think that other parts of Mono cost $$$ as well.

Something like NovellTouch, Touch.NET, CSharpTouch, etc. might work better.

- Demis
