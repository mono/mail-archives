While I’d *like* MonoTouch to be free as well (because it’s nice not to have
to pay for things, and I’m waiting for the price myself to know whether I’ll
be able to afford it), we have to be realistic and acknowledge the time and
resources that go into a development like this, and with such a tight
timeframe. This is not something you do in your spare time.
Any free option that was generous enough to be useful would also cannibalise
the paid option – which given the aforementioned development costs would
amount to asking Novell to take a loss. There needs to be *some* incentive
for them to develop development tools for a platform that is owned and
tightly controlled by another company (Apple).
I also think it's a good idea to keep things as simple as possible in order
to avoid any risk of running afoul of Apple's capricious approach to app
approval.
As for free as in speech as opposed to beer: in this case and at this stage
it might amount to much the same thing.
