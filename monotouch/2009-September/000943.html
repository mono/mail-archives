Ha Ha Lee, you are exactly right.
When I heard that the first of them got an iPhone I said "hey wait, you
can't even afford to pay your home telephone bill, why do you have an
iPhone".

It is sad but Mexican citizens carry more debt that any of us could possibly
imagine.

I used to work for a Debt Management / Education company and I asked them
why they didn't try to move into Mexico where people there really have debt
problems and they had this to say "that country is a lost cause... those
people will always be in debt".

In the United States we buy houses, cars and electronics we can't really
afford but we do so because we really "want" them, we don't really "need"
them. A lot of times we do it just to keep up with our neighbors and
friends.  Unbeknownst to us, our friends and neighbors all put themselves
into debt just to try to keep up with us.

It is no different in Mexico.

I have seen where people in Mexico can't afford to pay all of their bills so
they will typically not pay their phone bill because even if you don't pay
your phone bill, you can get it next month and keep the same number. Those
that have more problems than that may stop paying electricity and have a
neighborhood friend "hook'em up".

Sad but true, we all go into debt for our toys :-)

But I agree... let's forget about the economy in Mexico and focus on the
subject at hand... is their a way that Novell can bring this back... can
they set a price point that is more realistic and take hobbyist and
OpenSource Developers into account.

This beta has been filled with some great people.... I'm sure you all work
regular jobs but may also enjoy programming on the side for hobby, maybe you
even contribute to OpenSource Projects. That is great, but how many can
afford to toss $400 into a MonoTouch license to kick around at home?

Not many of us... believe me, I asked the wife and she said emphatically NO
:-)

By the way, I just want to take a moment to say thanks to all of the people
contributing to Mono, MonoTouch and MonoDevelop. you guys are great and this
has been one of the best betas I've ever seen.

Kudos to all!

Now let's get it priced to sell :D

-Kevin
