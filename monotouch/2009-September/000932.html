After looking at the pricing structure for MonoTouch I would have to agree
with everyone else, at this price point you will have a very hard time
selling this to anyone but businesses.
I know I will never be able to justify $400 to my wife.

I've been working professionally as a programmer for nearly 20 years and
I've rarely seen businesses that pay for Enterprise licenses, much less an
"Enterprise" license that is only good for one Developer.  There will be
tons of license abuse at this price point within businesses. Heck, most of
the places I have worked at outfit their Developers with Visual Studio they
got from one copy of the MSDN. I really don't see those types of businesses
shelling out $1,000 per developer for MonoTouch.

As everyone else has pointed out, the price for the "Personal" Edition
completely excludes hobbyist and Open Source Development which is a very sad
shame, especially since Mono and MonoDevelop were built by people that live
by the Open Source Freedom Philosophy.

I appreciate all the hard work from the Mono, MonoDevelop and the MonoTouch
Teams but at this price point I will just have to brush up on Objective-C.

For now, I would consider myself a hobbyist in regards to iPhone
Development.  Like all of us here, I would love to put an Application into
the App Store for the iPhone but with a full-time job and a family with
young children, I just can not justify paying $400 for a code library for
hobbyist programmer work when there are so many much more justifiable
expenditures for the family.

Let's remember that a vast number of the big success stories from the iPhone
App Store come from hobbyists programmers.

At this price point all I can say is good luck and thanks for a great beta.

-Kevin


On Thu, Sep 10, 2009 at 7:44 PM, Joseph Hill <jhill at novell.com> wrote:

> Hello all
>
> We're not making an official announcement regarding pricing and release
> dates, yet; however, we now have pricing established internally, and the
> team wanted to share this information since there have been a lot of people
> anxiously inquiring.
>
> Novell currently expects to launch MonoTouch next week.  Two editions will
> be available:
>
> MonoTouch Personal Edition:  $399/Developer
> A MonoTouch Personal Edition license is owned by an individual.  The
> license is non-transferrable, entitles the owner to one year of MonoTouch
> updates, and allows distribution of applications built with MonoTouch on
> Apple's App Store.
>
> MonoTouch Enterprise Edition:  $999/Developer
> A MonoTouch Enterprise Edition license is owned by a legal entity for use
> by one developer at any given time, entitles the owner to one year of
> MonoTouch updates, and allows enterprise deployments of MonoTouch
> applications, as well as the distribution of applications built with
> MonoTouch on Apple's App Store.
>
> The MonoTouch Enterprise Edition will also be offered as a 5-Developer
> License at $3,999 (a 20% discount).
>
> Please let me know if you have any questions about pricing, or about the
> distinction between the two products.
>
> Thanks for all of your help and feedback.
