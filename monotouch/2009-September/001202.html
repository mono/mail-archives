Hi Karl,

Thanks for replying. That section of the iPhone SDK agreement is  
actually a bit unclear on this matter - the context seems to imply  
that what is prohibited is the downloading of new code (it's in the  
same section that talks about plugins, for example). In other words,  
the intent *seems* to be that they are trying to make sure that the  
app that gets through their approval process is unmodified, as opposed  
to prohibiting how you implement the app.

This is, for example, the stance that the "rhomobile" people take (see  
question #8 in their FAQ - http://rhomobile.com/products/rhodes/61)

However, all of this might be a moot point if IronPython were to have  
some static compilation option. I'd be interested in IronPython  
support even if it came with certain restrictions (e.g. if AOT  
compilation made the language less flexible in some ways).

Take care,
-Dave
