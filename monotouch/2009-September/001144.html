On Tue, 2009-09-22 at 10:30 -0400, Simon Guindon wrote:
> I have thought about this as well however a standard iPhone
> application in MonoTouch is very much larger than its Objective-C
> counterpart and in danger of hitting the "over the air downloadable"
> limit of 10MB once you start adding images to your application. Much
> less head room.

Keep in mind that the 10MB limit is for *compressed* downloads.

For example, the OpenGLESSample-GV, compiled for device, is 7.9MB.
Compressed using 'zip', it's 2.1MB.  (In short, ARM assembly compresses
*really* well.)

So when looking at file size limits, make sure you're checking the
correct (compressed) file size.
