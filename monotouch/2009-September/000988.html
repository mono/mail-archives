1. mtouch isn't a compiler, it's a "driver", calling the appropriate
compiler for simulator or device output, running the linker, etc., etc.

2. mtouch is currently proprietary, and is one of the MonoTouch tools.

3. Just because something is documented at go-mono.com doesn't mean that
it's free.  The MonoTouch types are also documented there; see:

	http://www.go-mono.com/docs/index.aspx?tlink=root:/MonoTouch-lib

(That's missing some namespaces, though; expand the MonoTouch Framework
node on the left to see all of the MonoTouch namespaces.)

 - Jon
