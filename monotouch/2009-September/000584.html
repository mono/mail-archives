I think the value of XNA isn't for the top notch high end games, but rather
for smaller games where DX9 etc is more than sufficent, and its made by a
smaller team, or hobbyist. I don't know anything about the community , and
not much about the platform whether it really sucks or is the easiest
quickest game dev framework since sliced bread. To me i see the value is
that a small team  can make an app that runs on PC and XBOX with an XBOX
distribution mechanism that doesn't cost tens of thousands of dollars, and
the 2nd benefit is "run anywherish" when you see things running on
silverlight in the browser, ZUNE, and possibly monotouch. Its an economy of
scale value for hobbyist/casual games.
