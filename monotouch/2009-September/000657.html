On Fri, 2009-09-04 at 09:36 -0400, Jon Dick wrote:
> I think it would be possible with P/Invokes to call the native sqlite
> libs on the iphone.  That's a bit of a challenge to wrap them all up
> with pinvokes, and not very '.NET like' for consuming them, but it
> could be a solution for now.

That is, in fact, what Mono.Data.Sqlite.dll does.  The problems are
twofold:

1. Mono.Data.Sqlite.dll depends on System.Data.dll, which isn't
currently present in MonoTouch.

2. Once (1) is solved, there's still possible version incompatibilities
-- Mono.Data.Sqlite.dll targets SQLite 3.6.16, while iPhone includes
SQLite 3.0.  We don't know what, if anything, changed between these
versions.

In theory you could take Mono's System.Data sources, trim out everything
except the System.Data interfaces & System.Data.Common, bundle with
Mono.Data.Sqlite, and be good to go.

> I'm not aware of anyone explicitly saying they've tried the 100% c#
> sqlite implementation yet.  

That's an alternative solution that should be explored as well.
