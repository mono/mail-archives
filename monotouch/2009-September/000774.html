As I recall, it was previously stated that pricing hadn't been decided on
yet, but would likely be similarly priced to most commercial .Net component
packages.  Presumably Novell is (astutely) targeting existing .Net
developers, for whom they're used to that pricing.  My experience, having
spent the last 6-8 months working on an IronPython + Silverlight app is that
the pricing seems to be $800-1200, at least from the two vendors we looked
at /dealt with.
Resharper, which is a Visual Studio plugin (and while MonoDevelop is a solid
tool, MAN do I miss Resharper) for refactoring, etc. itself is $350  ($200
for a personal license - commercial licenses are licensed by company name so
you can transfer to other developers).  So, depending on how you want to
equate MonoTouch, you likely could be anywhere within that range.

FWIW (and if my opinion matters),  $800+ is, at least to me as an
independent developer (the IronPy stuff paid for by my employer), a bit
steep.   $300 would be the "sweet spot" For me, as something that I can
justify to myself as a reasonable cost of doing business.  Each person may
opine differently on that, but I figure I spent about $300 registering my
LLC in the last few months (so that when I publish apps it's not under my
given name, and for other reasons) and another $100 paying apple for a
developer account/the right to publish apps.

If I was building a professional application for my employer (or a client
was footing the bill), a $800-1200 product would make sense, presuming it
had a featureset that met that price point.  For example, I believe CoreData
isn't yet implemented in MonoTouch... Bindings like that might not be needed
all the time, but having them in a Pro priced product would be crucial to my
own consideration.

So, my two cents would be that for a basic package, with the tools needed to
build a solid app (keep in mind, as a friend recently pointed out, CoreData
is new in 3.0 and wasn't previously available so it's not a "required" api,
per se) ~$300 would be an ideal price point for an independent developer.  I
wouldn't be adverse to seeing a more full featured version, with a higher
price point.
