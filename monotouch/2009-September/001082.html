On Thu, Sep 17, 2009 at 7:36 PM, Brian Makuch <bmakuch at bitfoundry.com> wrote:
> I am following along in the Hello World tutorial and I'm having a
> problem with the "Using Actions" section. I have defined the action in
> IB linked the event to the app delegate. The partial method is correctly
> defined in the designer file. However, when I try to complete the
> partial in my application or export my own handler I get a warning that
> "The private method '<method name()>' is never used. (CS0169)".
>
> In the xib's xml, the connection record does show the button as the
> source of the event and the app delegate is the destination. I'n not
> sure what else I should look at? What could be the likely cause?

The C# action is connected to the widget by reflection at runtime, so
this warning is expected.

You can use "#pragma warning disable 0169" "#pragma warning enable
0169" around your actions if you want to suppress this warning just
for these methods, or add  0169 to the "Ignore warnings" field in
compiler options if you want to disable it for your own projects.
