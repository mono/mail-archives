That does make sense, in my opinion.

Of course, you are right. It is worth the price when it boosts your
productivity, but.....

There are many independent developers out there doing awesome work. But many
can not afford to spent at least 400$ to be able to use C# instead of Obj-C.
400$ is much money for a guy with family, no matter in which country you
live.

I think it is (at least at this point) more important to build a healthy
user group than making money from the first moment on. Maybe it would make
more sense to diversify the licensing. One that benefits the small
developers.

Something like this:

49$-99$ - Private use and only for free apps. (or only for open-source apps,
something the community would benefit from)
99$-149$ - After-work developers with paid apps.
399$ - One developer licence for companies.
3999$ - Company licence.

Just a thought!
