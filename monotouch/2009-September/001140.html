On 9/22/09, Lee Whitney <lwhitney at hdgreetings.com> wrote:
> What are most common cases where the linker can't do the optimal thing?

The linker is a tool that statically analyzes assemblies. So whenever
you need a type or a member that is not statically referenced, you'll
have to instruct the linker, either by linking the SDK only, or by
applying a Preserve custom attribute, to well, preserve the type or
the member.

Indeed, the most common scenario is reflection, and everything that
uses it, such as dynamic type instantiation, or serialization.
