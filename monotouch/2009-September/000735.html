> Hi,
>
> Ideally there would be e.g.
>
> 1) Personal - A bit like the IPhone SDK you can build and run it against
> the iPhone simulator but you can't deploy to a real phone or the app store.
>
> 2) Professional - You can deploy to a real phone and the app store
>
> 3) Enterprise - Pricing to reflect larger teams etc
>
>
> Being able to use C# and .NET libraries is a huge plus but like anything
> else we need documentation, samples, books, usergroups etc to make it easier
> to take what we know (C#) and apply it to iPhone development. Without this
> the effort of learning how to apply your C# skills may be more effort than
> learning Objective-C (and there are plenty of samples, docs etc to help get
> you started). This is one of the reasons why I have been adding links etc to
> http://monotouch.info to help others (and me as well) get to grips with
> how to apply what we already know.
>
> Content can not be generated by Novell alone so you really need a critical
> mass of devs contributing content, samples, presentations etc to make
> monotouch even more appealing.
>
> Option 1 would allow hobbyists and curious devs to try things out without
> any additional cost (since the iPhone SDK is free as well....o.k some of us
> have had to buy a MacBook :) ). If it isn't possible then an affordable
> personal license (e.g. I see the Unity pricing as too high for the
> curious/hobbyist) may help. If after playing with MonoTouch for a while they
> decide they've come up with an app they would like to deploy to their phone
> or to the app store they could then upgrade to the professional license.
>
> Option 2 would need to be competitive and option 3 would ideally have a
> range of options (to fit the needs of different companies)..
>
> Do I wish MonoTouch was free? Part of me says yes (the part that is
> currently curious about building iPhone apps with C#) but the other part of
> me says no (if I was building a commercial app I would want MonoTouch
> supported and funded via licensing so that dedicated people could be put
> onto the product). Mono, Moonlight and a bunch of other things have been
> made available and that's great but if MonoTouch is to become great (in a
> short time, support the iPhone API well and potentially more than one phone
> e.g. Android) I think it needs to make Novell money in order for them to
> justify investment in it (since I can't see MonoTouch helping Novell sell
> Linux licenses etc).
>
> So I understand the reasons for charging but hope they make the barrier to
> entry low enough that a large community grows up around MonoTouch.
>
> This is my personal opinion but I thought I would add it to this thread
> since the topic was being discussed.
>
> Cheers,
>
> John
