Put it this way: irrespective of anything else, if someone installs the SDK
on a Hackintosh today (in contravention of the Agreement, but that's not my
point), they should not be surprised and probably won't be in a position to
complain/seek redress if a subsequent version of the SDK fails to install or
shows reduced or compromised functionality (such as the simulator not
working for example), or if there are problems (other than the usual ones)
with iTunes Connect.

 

Or to put it another way: I wouldn't bet a business (real or potential) on
it.
