On Wed, Feb 9, 2011 at 9:08 AM, Miguel de Icaza <miguel at novell.com> wrote:

> > I've got a UIView subclass that needs to do some cleanup when it's no
> longer
> > needed (removing event listeners etc). I tried overriding Dispose(), and
> got
> > a compilation error. Fair enough; I wouldn't want people forgetting to
> call
> > base.Dispose() on their NSObjects either.
>
> The pattern is to override not the Dispose () method but the Dispose
> (bool disposing) method.   The flag that you get (disposing) tells you
> whether the invocation is happening because the user invoked the
> Dispose() method directly, or if this is being called as a Finalizer
> by the runtime (in a separate thread).
