On Fri, Feb 11, 2011 at 9:09 PM, David Moles <davidmoles at thegogame.com> wrote:
> Right now when my boss wants to see a new version of the app, he has to
> interrupt whatever I'm doing, wait for me to get the code in a runnable
> state, and have me deploy from MonoDeveloper to his phone. I'd like to set
> something up to do automated nightly builds for ad-hoc distribution, which I
> could then just archive somewhere. Has anyone done this, or have any
> suggestions of good tools for it?

You could write a script to call MonoDevelop's commandline tool,
mdtool, to do the build, then call other commandline tools to archive
(zip) and upload (scp) it somewhere. Then your boss can just download
the zip and install it through iTunes.

/Applications/MonoDevelop.app/Contents/MacOS/mdtool build --help
