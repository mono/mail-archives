On Sun, May 13, 2012 at 5:40 AM, Slicc <Steven_licciardi at hotmail.com> wrote:
> Is it possible to create a secure socket connection from monotouch. I
> understand that nettcp is not possible, but are there other options?
> Sslstream for example?

There's no suport for SSL/TLS on a socket (in MonoTouch, Mono or
MS.NET). It was a design decision (from MS). Working with streams is
easier and can be reused more easily (even if I don't recall any
non-socket "real life" uses).

> This needs to a persistent connection, no https.

SslStream is not HTTPS. It's used for HttpWebRequest (for HTTPS) but
also for database connections that requires SSL/TLS.

SslStream is SSL/TLS on a stream. If you create a NetworkStream on a
Socket then you'll effectively have a "secure socket connection".
