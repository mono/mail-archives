G'day mate

I try to stick with problem domains I'm familiar with or good at - 
meaning trying
to implement apps for stuff I do at work, or hobby etc.

Sometimes I look at other apps or better to say I start using them and 
see what they are missing and
I need - actually looking for niche.

Example: we do contribute to open source CMS system called Composite C1 
and we are about to
finish framework for integration of client devices (mobile or not) with 
Composite C1. The proof
of concept is News package. Sending from client few strings to CMS. 
Sorta personal Twitter and
we call it Chirper. The same stuff will be for Bugzilla package that we 
are developing, sending
Exceptions from client devices to CMS.

Then there is one app which is port from desktop app for 
controlling/monitoring broadcasting
transmitters. The idea is that operators are not standing by equipment 
24x7 and I suggested
to implement subset of features as mobile, but management does not 
understand what I want to
implement, so I do that in my spare time. Thanks to Mono* I can do that.

Basically I try to move some features (subsets) usually data acquisition 
and minimal number
crunching to mobile dev and share Model/Business-Logic code with server 
side.
This is dichotomy of today's two hype terms: mobile and cloud.
Basically I try to stay away from pushing features or UX meaningfull for 
desktop or server to
mobile and vice versa. I call this phenomena: technology-rape (Windows 
Mobile UX or today's
Metro on desktop)

cheers

mel
