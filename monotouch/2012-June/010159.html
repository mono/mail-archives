Chris,

I just did something like this today.  The way I did it was to add a UITabBar to my view in IB and then manually handle the tabbing myself (e..g tabBar.Selected +=….).  When a tab is clicked on I create a view controller and add it to the view (e.g. this.View.AddSubview(tab1ViewController.View) and hide whatever is the current view for the current tab, etc.  It works and I don't have to mess with swapping out a tab bar controller with a nav bar controller (in my case), etc.

Gerry
