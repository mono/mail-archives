You definitely want an objective C primer.  Everything you do in MT is what
you would do in Obj-C, just without the annoying memory management and
(IMHO) far better syntax and debugging tools.

   - You use XIBs and Interface builder.
   - You need to understand the application life cycle and how/when events
   fire.
   - The fundamental ideas of outlets and actions come from Obj-C

As I learned this weekend, the easiest port for a MT app isn't to WP7.
Porting back to Obj-C is much easier because the logic is the same.

-a
