Hi Steve,

I don't think that this tells us much as the current T.O.S are rather vague
and open to some interpretation (especially for imaginative stretchy minds
who want to fall within the rules).
What would be ideal is some indication of intent, or ideally if this means
that technology like MonoTouch are fundamentally prohibited.

e.g. Something along the lines of, 'do you intend to prohibit/kill all 3rd
party tools for developers programming with languages other than C/C++/Obj-C
from creating apps that can be deployed in the App store?'.
Also some details on whether the 3.3.1 clause was under review and likely to
change would also be informative.

The annoying thing is that the iPhone/iPad is locked and the App store is
effectively the only commercial opportunity developers have.

Anyway I don't think the hammer is all the way down, until Apple or Novell
make an official statement. Knowing Apple, it is likely we may not know
until the first MT app is rejected because it contravenes clause 3.3.1.

- Demis
