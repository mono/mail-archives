Thanks for this, the context of the replies is very telling.

There is only 2 reasons I can think of why they would issue the following
reply to your request on using MonoTouch.
“Our agreement is aimed at developers who want to distribute their apps
through the App Store “

1) MonoTouch and all other related technology is banned. But rather than
reply 'Yes our new monopolist strategy effectively renders your effort and
investments with MonoTouch futile' they choose to sugar coat it and make it
sound like it will only be limited via the 'App store channel' but we still
allow you the freedom to explore every other opportunity.

2) It is not a direct answer so they haven't fully made their intentions
clear and that they are likely processing the negative-sentiment and media
backlash before making there official stance.

Based on the wording in that reply, my conclusion is that MonoTouch is
currently prohibited but they may be leaving themselves room to reverse
their decision if it turns out to be a catastrophic one.

Regards,
Demis
