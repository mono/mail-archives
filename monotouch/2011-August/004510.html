When you start MonoDevelop, it checks your existing license if you have MonoTouch installed already. It will then compare your license to the serial on the machine. MonoTouch only does the license check when you first install it, from that point on MonoDevelop will check the license every time you restart the MonoDevelop IDE (and possibly even every time you compile MonoTouch-based code).
I think you can develop on Win7 MonoDevelop with MonoTouch based code as well, you just can't compile and deploy it until you switch to OSX.

I could be wrong, but this is the way I understood it to work.
