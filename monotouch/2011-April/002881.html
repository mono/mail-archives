I think the thinking around the student version is: its for students
to do projects with. If they then want to put it into the appstore,
they are no longer "learning", they are making money off it*, and
should really cough up for a license.

Given how flexible the novell guys are, if you bought the $99 one,
wrote an app (eg as part of your last-year of college or something)
and wanted to put it in the appstore, they would come to some
arrangement (most likely, you buy an upgrade for $299). you'd also
have to pay apple $99, BTW.

Just because it's not spelt out on the website doesn't mean it can't
be done. If you have a specific request, email Joseph Hill.

Also keep in mind that the Apple University program
(http://developer.apple.com/programs/ios/university/ ) does NOT allow
you to put your app in the store - or even do AdHoc stuff (it only
allows devices which are in within your "developer team" to be used).
So the Novell one is very much inline with what Apple has set up.

* if they decide to give the app away free, thats their decision. it's
still a commercial agreement with apple - and novell.

I suspect I also need to add: I don't work for Novell. I just use and
like MonoTouch a LOT. It's saved me many many many times the cost.....
