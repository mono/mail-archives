I've got an Obj-C API that takes a list (loosely defined) of CGImages and
another of CMTimes.

My first version of it took a pair of NSArrays, one of CGImages (cast to id),
the other of NSValues.

On the Obj-C side, AVFoundation provides a handy extension method on NSValue,
valueWithCMTime, to create the NSValue, and an instance method, CMTimeValue,
to extract the CMTime from it.

Unfortunately, I couldn't figure out how on the Monotouch side to populate
an NSArray with either CGImages or CMTimes, since neither inherits from
NSObject.

I tried rewriting the API method to take ordinary C arrays, and likewise the
corresponding Monotouch method to take a CGImage[] and a CMTime[]. But
btouch sees this and thinks it's supposed to create NSArrays out of them,
and it's no better at that than I am:

tmp/GoGame.iOS.AV/AVUtils.g.cs(140,52): error CS1503: Argument `#1' cannot
convert `MonoTouch.CoreGraphics.CGImage[]' expression to type
`MonoTouch.Foundation.NSObject[]'
...
tmp/GoGame.iOS.AV/AVUtils.g.cs(141,51): error CS1503: Argument `#1' cannot
convert `MonoTouch.CoreMedia.CMTime[]' expression to type
`MonoTouch.Foundation.NSObject[]'


I guess my workaround is to pass in an NSArray of UIImages instead of
CGImages, and do the conversion on the Obj-C side; and likewise to pass in
NSTimeIntervals instead of CMTimes.

But is there a straightforward way to pass in the Core Foundation types?
