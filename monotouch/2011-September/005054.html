> if I have understood correctly, MonoTouch.Dialog is usually used with the

> be good in iPhone applications, where you usually have a full-screen
> UITableViewController, but in iPad applications I see a lot of situations
> where a "table" created with MonoTouch.Dialog could be used in just a part
> of a form, so that a UIView could / should be used as container (you should
> just have one UIViewController in your screen, that works as a root for the
> other controls).
>
> I have been able to insert a MonoTouch.Dialog "table" in just a UIView (that
> is a small partition of the screen) creating a new class, starting from
> DialogViewController, removing all the parts regarding the
> UITableViewController and making it inheriting from UIView (and not
> UIViewController, for the reasons mentioned above).
