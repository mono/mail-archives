> 1) "mtouch --help" tells me that -nolink does not link assemblies. How
> does this help?

"Linking" assemblies is the process of *removing* all types & members
that your application doesn't use.  For example, if you never use
System.Diagnostics.Trace, then it will be removed from your application,
thus shrinking the application's size.  Smaller apps are nice
(especially considering that there's a 10MB limit for downloading apps
over 3G, iirc).

However, occasionally the linker removes too many members (for example,
System.Activator.CreateInstance() may be used to create a type, which
requires that the type have a default constructor; the linker must
preserve the default constructor, otherwise the call will fail).  In
these cases, you can use -nolink to skip the linking step.  Your app
will be bigger, but at least it will (hopefully) work.

Please tell us when the linker screws up so that we can fix it.  :-)

> 2) Do I still need -nolink in Beta 2?

Maybe.  If you run your app and it works, then you don't need it.  If
you get a System.MissingMemberException, then in all liklihood the
linker removed more than it should, and adding -nolink will fix it.
