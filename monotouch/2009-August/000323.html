> While as of yet there is no specified mime-type for protocol buffers,
> application/x-protobuf seems to be the most logical as far as telling
> the client via the Content-Type header that the response has been
> serialized in the protobuf format. 
>
>  
>
> FYI... After researching the current state of the protobuf mime-type
> conversation and realizing that there really hasn't been much
> discussion, and certainly no accepted resolution, I decided to post
> the following reply to the only thread I could find that touched on
> this subject in any detail: 
>
>  
>
> http://groups.google.com/group/protobuf/browse_thread/thread/540a09f87b60a402/88c4f9f73f5ff515?hl=en#88c4f9f73f5ff515
>
>  
>
> In short, my proposal is summarized by: Given that the usage of HTTP
> headers is, quite obvious, transport specific an effort should be made
> to adopt an transport agnostic-friendly solution that combines mime
> types with a format that has worked well for other dynamically driven
> spaces where name clashes are inevitable, that of using the
> tld.domain.classpath.type format adopted by the Java community to
> ensure that my application/foo+protobuf and your
> application/foo+protobuf can easily co-exist by simply using
> application/com.mydomain.foo+protobuf and
> application/com.yourdomain.foo+protobuf
>
>  
>
> Oh, and yes, I do recognize the fact that I both used the word Java
> and suggested adopting the classpath naming convention to a list
> filled with .NET heavyweights. My reasoning? I have yet to meet a .NET
> OSS developer who chooses religion over common sense. And to me,
> anyway, at least as it relates to this particular problem space, using
> the tld.domain.classpath.Type as part of a protobuf mime-type seems to
> make the most sense if your ultimate goal is to implement a solution
> that is transport-protocol agnostic.
>
>  
>
> Of course, this forum is obviously not the right place to be carrying
> on the 'what mime-type to use for protobuf's and when' conversation,
> so if you do have thoughts on the matter please subscribed to the
> protobuf-discuss group[1] if you're not already and respond directly
> to the thread mentioned above.
>
