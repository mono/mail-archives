> It would be nice to have at least the same templates as XCode, like the
> "flip-view" template etc. As regards having a Controller suffix, I'm not so

Ah, thanks, I should definitely take another look at XCode's
templates. I've mostly been going straight from IB, and it has a very
meagre set of templates.

> sure - it gives me a nice warm feeling of familiarity if the "code-behind"
> is just the front-end file with a ".cs" extension, just like in VS. People
> adopting MonoTouch/Develop are coming from the VS/.NET and having these
> little things as hints work wonders for ramp-up time, imho.

Yes, that's definitely why I went for the CodeBehind-like naming, but
there's a balance to be struck. If it feels superficially familiar but
behaves findamentally different, I'm not sure how much help that
really is.

Right now we have naming oddities like FooView.xib and FooView.xib.cs
resulting in a class called FooView, which is really a ViewController,
where the FooView.View member is the actual view. This naming doesn't
feel *quite* right to me -- even if most other aspects of this work
well.

I did consider appending a Controller suffix when first creating the
template, but I went with the current naming because I preferred it.
I'd just like to gauge whether I made the right decision :)
