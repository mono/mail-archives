> What that doesn't mention is two things of historical interest:
> 
>  1. The same guy developed both Cocoa# and the core idea behind the
>     MonoTouch bindings: Geoff Norton
> 
>  2. Cocoa# was unusable as a base for MonoTouch.
> 
> The reason behind (2) is that Cocoa# is based on top of
> System.Reflection.Emit (cf
> cocoa-sharp/src/Cocoa/Interop/ObjCMessaging.cs), and iOS platforms can't
> support System.Reflection.Emit (W^X architecture == no JIT ==
> System.Reflection.Emit is unavailable).  This fundamental platform
> constraint required changing how the ObjC bindings worked and lots of
> other interop "magic" you can find in the mono marshaling code.
> 
> For example, see the C# code generated by the MonoTouch btouch tool to
> see what is necessary to avoid System.Reflection.Emit calls:
> 
>         http://monotouch.net/Documentation/Binding_New_Objective-C_Types
> 
> Thus it's fair to say that Cocoa# influenced the design of MonoTouch,
> but there was not a direct relationship between the two.
>
