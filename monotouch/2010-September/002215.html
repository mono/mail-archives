Well the price is high (in my opinion) for being just a binding.


It is not just the binding.   The price includes the Mono runtime license
under a commercial, non-LGPL terms.

Mono has historically had very few monetization routes.   It is free on
servers, Linux and Mac desktops.   We only have actively monetized dual
licensing which stroke a balance between open source project and a few
avenues that would allow us to keep improving Mono.  This space is the space
where LGPL is not suitable for distribution.  This is a relatively small
market including certain classes of embedded systems and operating systems.

iOS happens to be one of the few OSes where you need this, and it is one of
the few spaces that we are able to monetize.

Miguel.
