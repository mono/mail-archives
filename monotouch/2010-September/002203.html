MonoTouch is awesome. I absolutely love MonoTouch.

In my opinion it's not fair to consider MD to be "free" in the context of
MonoTouch. In that context, as far as I am concerned MD costs $399. Xcode in
this context is completely free.

I haven't used Xcode since, well, MonoTouch came out. I have released two
apps in the app store writtenin ObjC using Xcode, and although it's often a
pretty simple IDE, I generally found it stable, usable and non-frustrating.

Now I am currently using the latest Mono (version 2.6.7), latest MonoTouch
(3.1.1.7912) and the latest MonoDevelop (2.4). I get a null reference
exception about every 5 minutes. This happens often while debugging, and
also on almost every single Go To Declaration call. Also in the past 2 days
I have gotten MD stuck in an infinite loop (beach ball that never goes
away). I first tried to kill it with the Finder, and although the Finder
considered the app dead the window persisted. The second time I killed it
with the kill command.

There are also lots of little nitpicks about MD that really add up, like how
you can't expand a unit test result (Run Unit Tests, in Test Result window
attempt to expand a result) without first defocusing MD and then refocusing
it. The same is often true with expanding tree nodes in the solution
explorer.

I'm not trying to be a jerk. I full well understand MD is a *giant*
undertaking. And all in all I actually think MD will become my favorite IDE
once the kinks are worked out. I also full well know what it's like to be on
a high pressure team that is expected to put out a lot of high quality code.
So don't get me wrong, I understand and don't think any less of the Mono
team because of MD's current state.

But it still should be known: if you are going to buy MonoTouch, you should
be aware MD is not quite ready for prime time, so buyer beware.


Matt
