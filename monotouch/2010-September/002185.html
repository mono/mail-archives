>From the top of my head:

   - Startup performance is a bit worse, since the application need to "load
   mono framework";
   - You might find bugs related to MonoTouch that you wouldn't find if you
   used only the native tools (not a big problem since the team working on
   MonoTouch is quite fast providing fix's or suggest alternative ways of doing
   the same thing);
   - MonoDevelop is not the most stable/complete IDE that you will find
   (even so, I still LOVE it :) )

And just to be clear about it : I love MonoTouch and the productivity that
it brings to me and my team. On a related note I'm starting to love
MonoDroid also :)
