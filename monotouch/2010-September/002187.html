One con might be that you have to learn Objective-C also in order to develop
with MonoTouch.  The reason is that most Google hits on questions that you
have will show Obj-C code, so you have to at least be able to read and
follow it.  This will change over time and already is.  That said, it¹s way
better to have to understand Obj-C than to actually program with it!

The only other complaint I have is tooling.  MonoDevelop is still pretty
crude compared to Visual Studio + add-ins.  Perhaps that¹s beside the point
because I can¹t really say how it stacks up to Xcode, having never used it.

You¹re probably struggling to find cons because MT rocks!
