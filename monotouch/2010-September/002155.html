On Thu, 2010-09-09 at 15:47 +0100, Matt Emson wrote:
> does that mean that Apple has relented on 
> interpreted code?

Presumably, as Lua and other interpreters were previously used by many
iOS applications and it was felt that it would technically have been
forbidden (even if no apps that I know of were actually blocked because
of it).

> I wonder if old school Mono will be allowed.

No.  The problem with regular "old-school" mono isn't the interpreter
(which mono doesn't have anyway), it's the JIT engine.  As mentioned
elsewhere, iOS is a W^X execution environment:

        http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/W%5EX

A JIT works by writing assembly code into memory, then executing that
memory, which is the exact scenario that W^X prevents.  Attempting to do
so anyway will result in the iOS kernel kindly abort(3)ing your app.

So, no JIT, and thus no "old-school" mono.
