> <snip>They saw the market need, began to apply their existing work

No, MonoMac was the reverse. It came out of the work for MonoTouch - 
indeed, AFAIR the tools used to create the bindings were based on the 
ideas/concepts that the MonoTouch implementation realised.

There were a number of C# Objective-C runtime and Cocoa bindings before 
MonoMac. There are at least one or two still actively being developed. I 
certainly still see traffic from the MonObjC guys occasionally.
