On Wed, May 4, 2011 at 15:29, Wally McClure
<theevilprogrammer at hotmail.com> wrote:
> Unfortunately, there is a problem.  The Mono team is a small team.  Everyone
> knows it.  Many customers are nervous enough as is when dealing with a small
> company, team, whatever.  You don't want to commit several developers to a
> significant amount of work (weeks, months, years, whatever) without an
> assurance that the work can bear fruit.  Now, add in the fact that there are
> several large companies looking at MonoTouch and Mono for Android.  Large
> companies tend to be very nervous about technologies not along the beaten
> path, and I would put the mono mobile products into that group.  Given that
> there now is a question in the marketplace, I do think it is appropiate to
> ask what is going on.  Since this story started up yesterday afternoon, my
> time, there hasn't been a public statement from anyone in an an important
> location at Mono, Novell, Attachmate, or otherwise besides a strangely
> worded statement of "all product roadmaps remain intact."  Given that:
>
> Many of us have committed the past 2 years to MonoTouch and have helped grow
> a community.
> Brian, Mike, myself and a bunch of coauthors, have dedicated significant
> time to write books on MT and M4A.  This isn't just our time, but the
> investment of time by publishers, editors, and marketing people.  These
> things effect other people.
> There seems to be a boogieman every time we turn around.  Whether its Apple,
> the techrights guy, or people inside of the same company that want to kill
> off their own company products, these are things that effect the community
> as whole.
>
> Now, I'm not complaining at any one person.  I'm just saying that given that
> this community of mobile developers has made a commitment to these products
> and are fairly passionate about them, it would be great to hear something
> directly from a person in the know and to have an official statement.
> Wally
