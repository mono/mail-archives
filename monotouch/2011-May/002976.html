As far as I'm aware, nothing has been announced yet, by anyone, except
the one article in internet news. From what I read - which may be
total BS - internet news isn't the most reliable of sources (I can't
find the reference to that now, tho, so I guess _I'm_ the unreliable
source now!)

http://blog.internetnews.com/skerner/2011/05/attachmate-lays-off-mono-emplo.html

So, until Miguel and co can and do say something.... it's all
speculation. I suspect, like most internal company stuff, even if they
DID want to say something, they can't. I've been in their position,
it's sucks not being able to tell customers whats going on.

Speaking of how things get lost in reporting, the original article says:

"Today Attachmate laid off an unknown number of U.S. based Novell
developers that were working on the open source Mono project."

Translation: 1 or more people. Actually, zero or more.

Slashdot, in it's usual style (ie, scaremongering and general uselessness):

"Attachmate Fires Mono Developers"
" The project is now at a crossroads, with news today that Attachmate
had laid off the US based development team for Mono"

Translation: EVERYONE working on Mono at Novell.

Now I see why /. is just a troll hotel. Thats not even close to the
original article, which they link so. So if we assume that the
original article is as far off the mark as the /. one is, then I guess
they just hired 10 more people....

<sigh>

(yup, I'd love to know if this effects MT and MD at all, tho given
they are paid-for apps, I suspect that the impact would be minimal....
but who knows.....)
