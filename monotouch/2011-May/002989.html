So, just in summary:

1. Attachmate has made NO public statements. See here:
http://www.attachmate.com/Press/Press+Home.htm (the acquisition only
just completed on the 27th!!)
2. There is NO reference in the "story".
http://blog.internetnews.com/skerner/2011/05/attachmate-lays-off-mono-emplo.html
- which makes it a rumour.
3. The actual quote - without any source - is this:

"We have re-established Nuremburg as the headquarters of our SUSE
business unit and the prioritization and resourcing of certain
development efforts - including Mono - will now be determined by the
business unit leaders there," said Jeff Hawn, Chairman and CEO of The
Attachmate Group in a statement sent to InternetNews.com. "This change
led to the release of some US based employees today. As previously
stated, all technology roadmaps remain intact with resources being
added to those in a manner commensurate with customer demand."

So, from that, assuming it's right:

1. dev efforts are determined by the BU leaders (no surprise there),
who are in germany.
2. they have let some US based employees go. No word on who or in what
areas. Remember Novell also has/had a big Suse division, I think. It's
not all Mono.

So, really, they could have "let go" 5 sales people in US offices, and
a few PA's, and hired 2 new people in the mono teams, while moving
some of the suse people to Germany (which is nice this time of year)

Unless he has info he's not published, thats a MASSIVE leap from "some
people in a fairly large organisation have been let go, and mono's
product management has moved under a german management team" to "all
of the mono people have been let go" (which is what /. said - f%^k I
hate /.)

puts the spooks up the market tho.
