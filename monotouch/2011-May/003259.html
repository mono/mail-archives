I'm going to say what I've said since the initial pricing was announced 
- it was overpriced and should have had tiered SKU's. The fact that Mono 
was encumbered with a commercial license for key elements and therefore 
stifled all "free" alternatives was horrible. Other projects using the 
LGPL have added a static linking clause, Novell chose not to.

I guess the most important thing now is - will this new venture own 
their own work, or will the Novell/Attachmate licence the Mono core 
elements that are LGPL to them? If the new venture has a commercial 
license for the elements that are not free to be statically linked, what 
reassurance is there going to be that the licensor will respect that 
license going forward. Especially in light of the direct competition 
that will happen upon release?!
