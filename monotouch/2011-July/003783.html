First up, here's what I've been recommending for a while:

1. Get the trial from monotouch.net, so you can dev in the simulator.

2. If you need to deploy onto a device, get hold of someone here (eg
me) and we'll try to arrange a ad-hoc build so you can put it on your
device (I think you get one device with a student version?). Eg if you
need to get it on a device for an end-of-semester project or
something. If it's just you hacking around, stick with the sim for the
moment.

3. Ignore MonoTouch otherwise - Attachmate bought Novell, and fired
all the developers (ie, Miguel, Joseph, Geoff, Michael and all the
others). They then formed Xamarin to make what I'm calling XamTouch
(and XamDroid, and maybe XamWebOS or XamBerry or XamSomethingElse),
tho I seriously doubt they will call it that.

Once they have XamTouch out, you will need to re-purchase a student
license - it's a separate company, and I wouldn't expect them to give
a discount for existing users. Actually, I hope they don't - I'd
rather they had MORE money to make MORE kick arse tools for me to use
:) Selfish? yes. I can deal with that :)

For more info, keep an eye on Miguel's blog: http://tirania.org/blog/
especially:

http://tirania.org/blog/archive/2011/Jun-28.html
http://tirania.org/blog/archive/2011/May-25.html
http://tirania.org/blog/archive/2011/May-16.html

With the usual disclaimers of "I dont have any inside knowledge",
Miguel said that a release of XamTouch would be out (at least in beta,
if not for purchase) "in 3 months" from mid-May (I think - can't
remember if it was 2-3 or 3-4). They have a lot of it working already
by the looks of it.

"Novell also pointed me to Xamarin (http://xamarin.com/)... Is this
the new MonoTouch...?"

WTF? That's like calling Microsoft about SQL Server and having them
say "oh, maybe you should call Oracle..." :)
