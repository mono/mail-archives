Terry, thanks for the link to OPAL (Open Physics Abstraction Layer)
(http://opal.sf.net).  I have contacted their developers and so far at
least one of them is willing to work with us (the RealmForge team) in
creating a C# port.  This will provide a very powerful object-oriented
physics layer with support for ODE first and other physics middlewares
(such as Newton Physics .NET and Novedex) later on.  It should be
independent of RealmForge and therefore usable with other .NET
projects that don't use it (or those that just use the Axiom rendering
engine directly). I hope that you will be involved with its
development once you have finished your latest Tao.ODE additions.

Would you be able to send me a copy of what you currently have of the
Tao.ODE source code whatever the state is after the next two weeks
(when development will begin on it) as it would be much better then
having to DllImport all the additionally needed functions ourselves.
