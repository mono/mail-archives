Well, the original motivation for Tao was game programming related as I 
understand it, but I always thought that definition was a little too 
narrow.  My interest in OpenGL bindings was motivated by an interest in 
scientific visualization (the first OpenGL app I ever saw was a 3d 
protein structure viewer on an SGI workstation).  The Ode bindings could 
be used for physics simulations outside of games, though probably 
nothing hard core.   I could probably think of non-game related uses for 
the other Tao bindings as well.  I wouldn't mind tweaking the definition 
of Tao a bit to de-emphasize the gaming connection a bit (or more 
precisely to include non-game uses in the definition of Tao).  I 
wouldn't have a problem with your bindings being part of Tao, myself.  
I'd like to see libflac bindings as well, actually.  The SDL bindings do 
provide access to libvorbis, but just for playback, I think.  Do your 
bindings provide support for encoding too, or just decoding?

Cheers,

T.
