> On the topic of the managed OpenGL bindings, that project sound very
> interesting. I am interested in helping out. Note that scope creep can be a
> challenge though. I can attest to that through my experience with Tao.Sdl
> and SDL.NET. On another note, would there be interest in having SDL.NET
> become part of Tao? Not merging the assemblies by any means, but maybe at a
> project level like Tao.OpenGl and the propsed managed GL library.

I'd have no problem having SDL.NET become part of Tao; I think we
should definitely encourage people to use well-written managed
wrappers as opposed to going straight to the metal of
Tao.Sdl/Tao.Gl/etc.  Avoiding scope creep for managed OpenGL will be
an interesting balancing act.. I can see the project easily spiralling
out of control to turn into an engine, as opposed to a set of helper
objects and methods that can be used by people writing engines.  I'll
write up some more of my ideas about the opengl.net stuff in a bit.

> On a project level, how does Tao fit in with the Mono project? Or more
> precisely, will Tao become part of a future Mono release?

I don't see any reason why not, but it's mostly a packaging decision. 
I'll talk to Miguel about it and see what he thinks.
