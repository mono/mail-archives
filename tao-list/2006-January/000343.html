Hi Daniel,

You mentioned Tao.Ode, so that's my cue to jump in.  It's probably not 
clear from the (minimal) readme files for Tao.Ode, but Trimesh 
collisions are a known problem area (mentioned in the 
Tao.Ode.Todo.txt).  I'll rework the docs to be a little clearer - they 
are out of date anyway since many things have been fixed since the 
original source infusion last summer.  Now that 'standard' collisions 
are working for everyone (that is to say the three people I know of 
actually using Tao.Ode <wink>), I'll move the Trimesh stuff to the top 
of my list.  I don't happen to have a trimesh collision example on hand, 
so if you want to save me some time, a concise test case or example is 
always welcome.

As an aside, I updated the trimesh collision callback delegates during 
the recent round of fixes, though they remain untested.  If you're on 
Windows, you might want to grab the latest source and recompile to see 
if it works better for you.

Cheers,

Terry
