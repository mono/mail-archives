Hello,

Now that I have a bit of time, I thought I'd jump into the conversation.

Although I've only recently jumped into the Tao League, I think I have 
worked with you guys enough to know that whatever happens, Tao will most 
definitely still live on.  My experience with both Dave and Terry in 
SDL.NET, OdeDotNet, and newly added Tao.PhysFs, have shown that both of 
you are very competent programmers and team leaders.  But confidence in 
the survival of the framework due to its team members and leaders isn't 
all that's needed with Tao, what is needed is something else.  Some of 
these issues have been mentioned before, but I'll just go over what my 
thoughts are:


Developers

Although the current system is good and stable, it is completely 
dependent on the head maintainer of the project.  One person is 
responsible for maintaining the developer list, adding new developers to 
a project that he isn't truly involved with.  It would be easier on not 
only the maintainer, but also the other developers to have the ability 
to let other developers add new people to the project.


Releases

The release schedule is another thing that is completely dependent on 
the head maintainer.  One big release comes when you, Vlad, have the 
time to make the release.  In order to speed up deployment of the 
projects, releases could be made independent from the entire project.  
That way maintainers could make releases of stable branches without 
having to wait for the next big Tao release.  But this also means that 
every project would be independent of Tao, meaning that every project 
may not have the same format as the rest of the framework.  Standards 
will have to be declared.

Another option is to have both systems implemented.  Have a major Tao 
release scheduled every two months or so by anyone who can afford to do 
a build and upload it as well as allow individual project maintainers to 
release their own project individually from Tao.


Website

Although this is only minor, I think it's still worth mentioning.  Many 
consider Tao's scope to be extremely small as it just provides low-level 
bindings, but this is untrue.  Tao has a huge user interest and this was 
demonstrated back when Tao has its own forum.  Giving Tao its own 
website would centralize Tao related content and give users more 
information about what Tao provides as well as what needs help, 
development-wise.

A forum might also be something that might need discussion.  Although 
mailing-lists are sufficient and provide a good communication tool and 
bring the discussion straight to your mailbox, they aren't that good for 
large discussion groups.  Having a dedicated forum would allow also 
discussions of different projects to be parsed into different forums 
(different forums for Tao.OpenGl, Tao.Sdl, Tao.Ode, etc.).  This is 
definitely something that might spark some interest among Tao users.


Well, that covers the key issues I have with the current system.  It's a 
shame that you don't have the time to maintain this, Vlad.  Although I 
won't volunteer to become the maintainer of the entirety of the Tao 
Framework (Terry and Dave are much more competent as maintainers then I 
am), I will volunteer to help out as much as I can to help move Tao over 
to whatever new system is implemented.  Whatever happens, Tao will live on.

Thanks a lot,
