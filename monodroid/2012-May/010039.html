Don't worry about this type of stuff guys.  I've been on both sides of the product line, so there are reasons for it.  People on the product side are tired of being asked the same question a bunch of times.  I never answered back "It will be ready after I have some sleep and am confident that it is going to be a successful product" but I thought about it several times.  If the Jonathans' (and others) screamed out at us, they would not offend me.  Product producers are (should be?) driven by quality goals. On the client side, consumers are driven by dates.  When will something be available.  Customers, editors, and such want to know when.  Trust me, I've been on this side as well.  I just went through this last fall with Wrox. They don't like having to push things back either.  Some customers just aren't comfortable with a product that says alpha or beta on it
 There's obviously give and take in all of this.   Wally Date: Thu, 3 May 2012 09:30:56 +0100
