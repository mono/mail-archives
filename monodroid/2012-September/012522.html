Hi!
you can do just STartActivity(typeof(frmMainMenu)

it's a short hand that's way sweeter.

Two, finish is for an activity, not an application. There is a way to shut
the application down, but it's not recommend by anyone ever. I would be
smart and keep track of your activities and send finish to each one when
you want this to happen. A cool and easy way to do this would be to set up
a broadcast receiver in each one (DO NOT FORGET TO UNREGISTER IT-- unless
you like weird error messages) and broadcast the finish command.

Another way would be to keep a list of your activities, and call finish on
each one you would like to close.

if you want quick, dirty, cheap not recommend method:

System.exit(0);  (though it's heresy on the internet as to why it's
not recommended. Sometimes it doesn't

work and you have to kill your own pid. I say  was was said above
about letting android handle your
application in this regard is smart. Manage your activities some how.
Find an example of how to inherit from the application class. Then in
oncreate of each acitiviy,

register yourself with your application class (like a list). in the
onDestroy (or if someone wants to suggest elsewhere) remove
your activity from that list. When the user hits the button you were
talking about, loop through the

activities, and Finish on them.

just my two cents and 3 suggestions.
