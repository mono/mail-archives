Not to answer for the team (and I'm sure they will let you know their
position) but creating custom project in VS2010 compared to VS2008 is much
much easier. Since the Mono team have developed MonoDevelop, it makes sense
to make this the secondary IDE of choice on Windows since it is free, open
source project and actively being worked on by the team.

"But MonoDevelop is unstable" I hear you cry; well, the more people that use
it, the more exposure it gets, the more bugs tracked with useful use cases
the more these sorts of issues can get sorted and the better product
MonoDevelop will become. If people avoid it because it's apparently
"Unstable" then it will never improve.

This all being said, if there is a need for VS2008 support and this is shown
by numbers of people requesting VS2008 support then I'm sure the team will
at least look into the possibility of doing it.

Hope this helps,
