Jonathan is right.  One encoder (the windows one) chose to encode the '+'
sign inside the shift sequence started at byte 4 (ending at byte 13).  The
mono encoder puts the + sign outside the shift sequence (the "+-", bytes 11
and 12 decode to just a '+')

You can check out the spec at http://www.faqs.org/rfcs/rfc2152.html if
you're curious.  Specifically Rule 2 of the UTF-7 definition.
