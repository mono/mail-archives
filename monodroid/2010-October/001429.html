Supporting VS2008 was a decision we had to make pretty early on.  We 
knew we had to support VS2010, and MonoDevelop for those without full 
versions of VS or on Mac and Linux.  Adding a third IDE would have 
required a significant amount of additional resources.

Because we chose early on to only support VS2010, we were able to take 
advantage of many new features in VS2010 for supporting "alternative" 
frameworks, allowing us to get to this state much quicker.

Jonathan
