On Wed, Aug 25, 2010 at 2:06 PM, Manfred Pohler <Manfred at pp-p.net>
wrote:

>From my point of view obfuscation makes sense when you build "rocket
science".

The only "safe way" is possible on systems which allow to bring in
"native code" in the meaning of external DLLs which 

hook deep into the system like "dongle protection" works.

You can "hide" your code by refactoring it to a hard readable thing, and
the result will be hundreds of (meaningless named) classes, 

methods, properties and interfaces a "thief" has to work through.

 

But the real thing that could be stolen is (in most cases) the
application itself.

Why would I steal - I want to have a good (selling) application with
little work.

And what makes a good selling application (excluding marketing)?

The idea (what it does), the design in the form of how things are
organized

and how they look (graphics, animations) and feel (sounds, UI elements).

To hide this you must hide you application - in other words: don't sell
it J

 

I don't know how your projects are done - but in our cases, design (UI
and all the things) is

much more work than coding.

Expect people with (good) programming skills when you talk about
"thieves". And all they have to know

is how it looks / works. They are for sure able to write their own code
to make a "blueprint" of your app.
