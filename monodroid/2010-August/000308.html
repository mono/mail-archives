Rafael and others, I do apologize if my message ruffled some feathers but
the facts are there. After the question was raised of "how much can we
show", the pertinent parts of the NDA were copy/pasted into the response
thread and Miguel even chimed in to make sure everyone understood why Novell
does not want us talking about MonoDroid publicly at this time yet if you
follow the thread after those points were made, you will find that people
are still asking "but what if" questions.

That's ok, I understand. I think it would be awesome to have a UG meeting
discuss programming for the big 3 mobile platforms using C# and .NET but the
NDA and Miguel have both made their feelings on the subject quite clear.

What led me to send the message I did was that there is another discussion
thread going on where someone has posted an intro to MonoDroid blog article
and based on Tweets, the same person plans a discussion at an upcoming User
Group meeting and even today another person is asking about interest in a
User Group for mobile development including MonoDroid.

In that last case I have talked to that person and they are merely trying to
drum up interest for a future UG and most likely not during the beta but
with all the talk I felt it necessary to try to get a once and for all "this
is what you can and can't do" statement from the MonoDroid team.

While I personally think we already got that, there are still questions
being posted and some who have already read the official responses and are
still trying to get a more clear answer.

Sorry if I came off rude but I, as well as many of us would like to get a
full, crystal clear answer on the subject.

Personally I think it would be acceptable to do a talk at a local UG about
MonoTouch and Windows Phone 7 development and mention that MonoDroid is
coming but I would think it would be unacceptable to fire up Visual Studio
and whip out a demonstration of MonoDroid at this time because as we all
know, stuff don't work as we expect sometimes during early betas like this.
We wouldn't want to give the wrong impression about MonoDroid.

If MonoTouch is any indication of where MonoDroid is going, I think it's
safe to say that we will have a winner on our hands but it is best to follow
Novell's wishes during the beta.

-Kevin
