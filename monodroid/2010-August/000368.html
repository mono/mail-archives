To go along with Tom's suggestion though, I have found that leaving the
Emulator does speed things up but often times when running a new project it
will die during deployment to the emulator.

If this happens just go into Settings+Applications (within the Emulator) and
Uninstall MonoDroid and the app or apps you were testing and then try again.

This was happening to me quite consistently today during my MonoDroid
lunchfest :)

I was going through samples and it would work fine on the same project but
then when I moved to another project it seemed to deploy fast since I left
the Emulator up and I could check Settings within the Emulator and the app
did indeed get installed but it never ran. Removing everything made it work
just fine.

-Kevin
