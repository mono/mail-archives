Insert ye standard "'Reply-To' Munging Considered Harmful" answer:

http://www.unicom.com/pw/reply-to-harmful.html

I myself hate Reply-To munging because when I don't want to reply the
list it increases the amount of work I need to do.  Plus, there are
times when the list has multi-day delays [0], so sending two emails via
two different return paths will help ensure that the response is
received.

 - Jon

[0] There are times on mono-list when I first read the email a week or
more after the email was sent.  I don't know why it was held up
(moderation?), much less why it was held up for so long, but it does
happen.  Do you really want a "crucial" reply to be held up by the whims
of the listserv?
