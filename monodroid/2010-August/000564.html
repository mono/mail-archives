I have 2 Android phones (a G1 and DroidX) which I use for app testing,
and the G1 was also used for system-level programming. It also gives
me a GSM and CDMA phone to play with, in case if there's differences
in some of my apps. Never had problems with app testing on a real
"this is my main phone" phone, since the app level stuff is nicely
sandboxed from the system. Worst is a battery pull and a data clear,
and using Astro to restore a backup of my apps.

Another option I'm looking into is some of the ARM based SoC machines
(I'm looking at the BeagleBoard-xM for some software/amateur radio
usage). Many are OMAP based machines, which is what the Motorola Droid
family run on. Downside is the lack of a cell modem...which may or may
not be important to your apps.

Another option, if MonoDroid and Android would support it, is running
on X86. Implementing this is left as an exercise to the reader.

But if you had to run out and buy, snag a used N1 and if your app
needed to interface the android.telephony APIs, a cheap prepaid SIM. I
know T-Mobile usually runs some disgusting specials on prepaid SIMs
from time to time, as seen by my stack of cheap Nokias.

Take Care,
Mark Crichton
