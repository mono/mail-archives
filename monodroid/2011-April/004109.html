On Apr 5, 2011, at 1:27 PM, Aaron Knabb wrote:
> Your explanation makes sense, but how does this apply to objects that don't have a dispose?

My explanation applies to all types which have a Java-side counterpart, for which the two "halves" need to be kept in sync. Thus, this applies to Android.App.Activity, but not to System.String, System.Collections.Generic.List<T>, or any other type that doesn't inherit from Java.Lang.Object.

Also note that calling Dispose() does _not_ collect the object. It instead "breaks" the connection between the Java & Mono GC's, allowing each VM to collect their respective objects. Without the Dispose() call, they will still be collected, it would just take longer for the cross-VM cycle to be found and disposed of.

Consequently, it _may_ be advisable to call GC.Collect; see also:

	http://mono-android.net/Documentation/GC
