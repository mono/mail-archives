For running the emulator quicker, here is a technique that I use that seems
to boost it significantly enough (my test drawing moved from 5 fps to 20+
fps). Your results might vary. Once the emulator is up and stable (before
you deploy/debug), go under task manager, processes, find "emulator.exe
*32", right click and change the priority to high. I usually lock it down to
one CPU (affinity) that is a lower used CPU. This is on a Core I7, which I
still can't believe that I would have to adjust the settings like this.
There is a risk of destabilizing the emulator or your development
environment.
