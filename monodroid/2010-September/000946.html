Please don't get me wrong. Simple is always preferred over complex, and
product before infrastructure most certainly. But after having who knows how
many randomly generated passwords coupled with a generic and global username
that is related to something else entirely it can get frustrating when you
find yourself spending what seems like far too much time just trying to
figure out how to gain access to the bits before actually being able to do
anything with them.  I'm happy to create the ASP.NET. If it gets used and it
proves useful, cool. If it doesn't and/or proves to be a pain in the ass,
I'm the one to blame.
