The attributes for the AndroidManifest.xml look good (one pascal casing
google does like), however wouldn't this be a bit confusing for people who
are use to naming their application via the project properties and
AssemblyInfo?

On Wed, Sep 15, 2010 at 1:50 PM, Jonathan Pryor <jpryor at novell.com> wrote:

> On Wed, 2010-09-15 at 13:39 +0200, Jonathan Dickinson wrote:
> > ·         Any .Net stuff is transformed into the [horrible] Java
> > naming convention. So even though my class/namespace is
> > “SourceCode.TaskDroid.Authenticator.Authenticator” MonoDroid exports
> > it as “sourceCode.taskDroid.authenticator.Authenticator”.
>
> What's really annoying is that we can't "fix" this and just
> export .NET-style names to Java.  Android goes out of its way to prevent
> PascalCased-package names; see:
>
>        https://bugzilla.novell.com/show_bug.cgi?id=634068
>
> We are thinking of ways to improve this, e.g.
>
>        https://bugzilla.novell.com/show_bug.cgi?id=632611
>        http://monodroid.net/Tutorials/Working_with_AndroidManifest.xml
>
> The specifics still need to be hashed out, though; instead of [Name],
> I'm currently thinking we'll have an [Activity] attribute with Name,
> Label, etc. properties.  While you'll still need to camelCase your
> package names, at least you'll have control over the names instead of
> needing to accept whatever monodroid produces:
>
>        [Activity(Name="foo.bar.Example", Label="My App!")]
>        class MyActivity : Activity {...}
>
>  - Jon
