Settings->Wireless & Networks->Wi-Fi Settings->(Menu)->Advanced->Wi-Fi Sleep
Policy->"Never when plugged in" or simply "Never" depending on your setup
should also alleviate this problem.

Alternatively, you can leave the Wi-Fi sleep policy alone, but then set your
screen sleep policy to never turn off while plugged in:
Settings->Applications->Development->Stay awake

On 17 January 2011 14:24, Aaron Knabb <aknabb at mobileepiphany.com> wrote:

> I thought I'd drop a little tip in here for other folks. If you are trying
> to debug on certain devices (noticed it on a moto droid), it appears that
> the wifi interface on the device likes to shut itself off or go to sleep.
> This makes it really dodgy trying to attach the debugger. To get around
> this, you can set up a constant ping to the device. This seems to keep the
> wifi "awake".
>
> Here's the windows command I ran:
>
> ping -t [IP of your device]
>
> After setting that up, I have a LOT more success device debugging.
>
