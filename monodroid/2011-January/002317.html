Yes I second this suggestion.  I don't think people would mind paying a fee for commercial use *if* they could develop with it for free non-commercially, and I for one like to be able to build and fully test an app before committing money to get a commercial licence.
Given that this software is likely to be very appealing to the Enterprise, you could offer a licence structure whereby it's, say, free for non-commercial use, $99 for indie developers (with a turnover of less than $100k) and $399 for organisations with a turnover greater than $100k.
