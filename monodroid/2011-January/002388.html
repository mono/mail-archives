On Jan 7, 2011, at 10:38 PM, A. Logan Murray wrote:
> However, when it tries to copy the application, it tells me there is not 
> enough space on the device!

http://monodroid.net/Documentation/Troubleshoot#There_is_not_enough_storage_space_on_the_device_to_deploy_the_package

Either VS is starting the emulator incorrectly, or you're manually launching the emulator.

Either way, exit the emulator, and launch the emulator manually, providing the `-partition-size 512` options.  Your app isn't installed to the SD card (by default), and thus the size of the SD card is irrelevant; what's important is the size of /data/data, which -partition-size controls.  Without that option, you get an impressively small 32-64MB /data partition (iirc).

> Looking in the /bin folder I see a few files: my main project .dll file, 
> which is 6 K, but then there are a couple .apk files that are roughly 12 
> megs... what is this, and why is it so huge?

.apk files are .zip files.  Unzip and find out. :-)

The .apk is huge because it contains all your assemblies, uncompressed: 

	http://monodroid.net/Releases/Previews/Preview_7#Packaging_Changes

One of these assemblies is Mono.Android.dll, which is huge (and responsible for most of your 12MB file size).

A future MonoDroid release will enable the linker, which should drastically shrink the size of the Mono.Android.dll which is packaged with your application.
