As a MonoTouch developer I would hope there is a bundle discount.  I had to
fight to pay the $999 per developer to license the Enterprise version.  It
really hits hard for a small dev shop like mine.  We are having to ramp up
developers two months at a time to spread out the license fees in our penny
pincher economy.

I would love to see a device limit license model.  I think MonoTouch could
use that too.  Maybe a per developer price based on the number of devices
you wish to deploy to.  Therefore you hit the educational market, the small
dev shop building proof of concept applications, and the marketplace
developers.

So for example:

1 - 10 devices $99
11 - 50 devices $299
51 - 500 devices $499
Unlimited $999

Make it so that a developer can increase the license by paying the
difference to up the user count.  This breaks down the cost barrier.  I know
it makes licensing more difficult, but it makes adoption easier.  It is much
easier for me to get your product in the door of my dev shop at a $99 fee.
Show off what it can do and then ratchet up the license when my VP goes gaga
over the efficiency of using your tool.

David Sandor
