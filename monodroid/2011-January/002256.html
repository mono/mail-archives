On Jan 6, 2011, at 9:04 AM, Josh Bartley wrote:
> I think the big issue is the mindset going into developing with Monodroid that you expect a System.Windows.Forms control.

To be fair, you hit the exact same problem with .NET Compact Framework (WinForms is very different, and comes in two sets to boot!), with MonoTouch (MonoTouch.UIKit), and with WP7 (Silverlight née WPF/Light, not WinForms).

Which basically means it's a "pervasive" problem, but one that should still be addressed. :-)

Consequently, I would highly suggest that any book covering MonoDroid (and MonoTouch, and WP7 for that matter) discuss MVC/MVVM/etc. EARLY ON and follow it in all examples.  Keep the UI separate from core logic, etc.  You're shooting yourself in the foot if you design your entire app around a single UI toolkit and find out you want to port to another platform.

 - Jon
