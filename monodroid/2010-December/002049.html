Hi Byron,

I've been asked this question before and there are many pages on the
subjects including Wikipedia.

http://www.veridicus.com/tummy/programming/java_vs_csharp.asp

http://geekswithblogs.net/CISCBrain/articles/Mono_vs_dotNet_Performance_Test.aspx

Regarding the database I would look into SQL Lite which is the default
database for Android and although text based is very fast due to the engine
being generated around in memory architecture, or hash tables and dynamic
loading to memory for caching and performance.

The biggest issue with Monodroid that I'm aware of is the embedded run-time
within your package, which does carry an overhead. The other being that it
is still in development and moving at an alarmingly fast rate, there are
still some aspects that have not fully been covered.

I wish you every success in making the right choice, I personally have used
Monodroid and keep a watch on the ximian mail list but have found that the
Native Android SDK with the Eclipse environment has been adequate for my
purposes.

http://developer.android.com/sdk/index.html

http://developer.android.com/sdk/eclipse-adt.html

Best Regards,

Glen
