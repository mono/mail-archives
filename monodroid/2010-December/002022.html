On Dec 6, 2010, at 3:30 AM, Carlo Bolz wrote:
> Ok, thank you for the hint. 
> But my piece of code throws an exception. What I´m doing wrong?

What you're doing wrong is (possibly) twofold:

1: This:

>            locManager = (LocationManager)GetSystemService(LocationManager.GpsProvider);

is incorrect, and returns null (hence your NullReferenceException).  The correct code is:

	locManager = (LocationManager)GetSystemService(Context.LocationService); 

2. You need to add the 'android.permission.ACCESS_FINE_LOCATION' permission to your manifest, otherwise your subsequent `locManager.RequestLocationUpdates()` call will throw a SecurityException; see also the LocationManager.GPS_PROVIDER documentation:

	http://developer.android.com/reference/android/location/LocationManager.html#GPS_PROVIDER

You can do this by editing Properties\AndoridManifest.xml and adding a /manifest/uses-permission element:

	<?xml version="1.0" encoding="utf-8"?>
	<manifest xmlns:android="http://schemas.android.com/apk/res/android" package="package.name">
	    <uses-permission android:name="android.permission.ACCESS_FINE_LOCATION" />
	</manifest>

You may also want to add:

	<uses-feature android:name="android.hardware.location.gps" />

but this doesn't appear to be required on my N1.
