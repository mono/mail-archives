> We're still evaluating it's use. A couple things though about our
> application make an ORM like nhib worth looking at:
> 1) our application is very large, so any code bloat is trivial
> 2) our application has a large and complicated data schema, so a
> full-featured ORM will save us a lot of headache
>
> Performance with nhib for standard data calls doesn't provide much
> overhead, even on the device. In fact, on the device it's quite fast (not so
> much in the emu).
>
> The one thing that we've found that's slow is the initial ISessionFactory
> creation. This only has to be created once per app domain though (in
> practice, this means once, when the application is cold launched). And even
> for that, we're investigating extending the nhib code to allow serialization
> and caching of the sessionfactory.
