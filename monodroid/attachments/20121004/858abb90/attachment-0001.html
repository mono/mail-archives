<p class="MsoNormal"><span style="font-size:11.5pt;font-family:"Arial","sans-serif"">Greetings, Developers!</span><b><span style="font-size:10.0pt;font-family:"Times","serif""><br>
<br>
</span></b><span style="font-size:11.5pt;font-family:"Arial","sans-serif"">Today we are announcing the launch of the Xamarin Forums:
</span><b><span style="font-size:10.0pt;font-family:"Times","serif""><a href="http://forums.xamarin.com/"><span style="font-size:11.5pt;font-family:"Arial","sans-serif";color:#1155CC;font-weight:normal">http://forums.xamarin.com</span></a><br>
<br>
</span></b><span style="font-size:11.5pt;font-family:"Arial","sans-serif"">You can use the new forums to give us feedback, demo your projects, share cool tips and tricks, talk about the latest announcements, and discuss other relevant technical and community
 topics. Think of it as a perpetual meetup in the cloud, a place where you can kick back and rock out with other members of the Xamarin community. The mailing lists will continue to operate in parallel with the forum for the foreseeable future, but we aren't
 going to promote Nabble anymore or attempt to integrate the lists into the new forums.</span><b><span style="font-size:10.0pt;font-family:"Times","serif""><br>
<br>
</span></b><span style="font-size:11.5pt;font-family:"Arial","sans-serif"">We’re using the excellent forum system from the folks at Vanilla Forums. It offers some great features, including in-site and email-based notifications of new posts and threads, a global
 activity feed, a badge system (à la Foursquare), and support for real-time private messaging. It’s cooler than a barrel of code monkeys, and we’re inviting you all to join today. You can sign in with your Xamarin account - the same one that you use on the
 Xamarin store. If you don’t have an account yet, you can register one here: </span>
<b><span style="font-size:10.0pt;font-family:"Times","serif""><a href="https://auth.xamarin.com/account/register"><span style="font-size:11.5pt;font-family:"Arial","sans-serif";color:#1155CC;font-weight:normal">https://auth.xamarin.com/account/register</span></a><br>
<br>
</span></b><span style="font-size:11.5pt;font-family:"Arial","sans-serif"">After you join the party, be sure to check out Nat’s forum kickoff thread:
</span><b><span style="font-size:10.0pt;font-family:"Times","serif""><a href="http://forums.xamarin.com/discussion/12/xamarin-forums-kickoff"><span style="font-size:11.5pt;font-family:"Arial","sans-serif";color:#1155CC;font-weight:normal">http://forums.xamarin.com/discussion/12/xamarin-forums-kickoff</span></a><br>
<br>
</span></b><span style="font-size:11.5pt;font-family:"Arial","sans-serif"">You can also tell us a bit about yourself in the introductions thread:
</span><b><span style="font-size:10.0pt;font-family:"Times","serif""><a href="http://forums.xamarin.com/discussion/13/introduce-yourself)"><span style="font-size:11.5pt;font-family:"Arial","sans-serif";color:#1155CC;font-weight:normal">http://forums.xamarin.com/discussion/13/introduce-yourself</span></a><br>
<br>
</span></b><span style="font-size:11.5pt;font-family:"Arial","sans-serif"">Feel free to start your own thread in any relevant section. We’re looking forward to seeing some great conversations. We also plan to use the forum as a way to facilitate discussion
 on the topics we raise on our blog. You’ll soon start to see forum discussion thread links in many of our blog posts, much like the link below. We welcome any suggestions that you have for improving the forums and making them a more effective communication
