On May 5, 2011, at 1:51 PM, Neal wrote:
> Will we be required to refresh Mono for Android every 6 months in order to continue using this product?

No.

> When building my app I see a warning that the signer certificate expires in 6 months.

In order for a package to be installed on the device, it needs to be signed with a certificate. To simplify use, Mono for Android generates a "debug" certificate for your app during the build process (as `ant` also does with the Java toolchain). This means that you can't deploy your debug app to the app store (which you can't do anyway).

You can select a different certificate by either signing yourself or editing the .csproj:

	http://mono-android.net/Documentation/Build_Process#Signing
	http://mono-android.net/Documentation/Guides/Preparing_Package_for_Android_Marketplace#Step_4_-_Sign_the_Package

> If not, please consider this hopefully rare possibility but not to have the software ever expire.

Mono for Android does not expire.
