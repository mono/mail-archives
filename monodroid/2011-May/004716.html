I think we can feel a little optimism guys.  If people were getting laid
off, they'd hardly have a motive to keep silent.  I guess there's severance
potential, but you got to figure that at least one guy out of the group
would be noble / mad enough to take the risk and leak the information
through a trusted channel or something...

It seems more plausible to me that instead what's going on is a pretty hard
core dialog about the future of mono, how it relates to suse and the germany
thing, and that if anyone leaks any of that data out, then they could be
fired, so the motivation is greater on the employees part to keep mum.  heck
- maybe they're going to sell mono to canonical or something, and they have
to keep hush hush untli that deal goes through... who knows???

this is sort of a Schrödinger's black box kind of thing...

let's hope I'm more right than not!!
