Great! Good luck, Dean!

FYI for other people considering dev phones, the LG Optimus T is a great
minimum performance device, but if you want higher end ones, I'd recommend
either the T-Mobile G2 if you need something with a keyboard, or the
T-Mobile G2x if you need something with the multi-core platform. For
tablets, the G-Slate is just as good as the XOOM, and slightly cheaper too.
There's also the obvious: the Nexus S.

That's not to say that other devices aren't as suitable. However, my
personal experience is that those particular devices are nearly hassle free
when it comes to all types of development for mobile devices, Android or
otherwise.

I wouldn't really recommend any AT&T phones except MAYBE the Samsung
Captivate (and the Samsung Infuse 4G), but if you must have an AT&T phone,
the Captivate and the Infuse are your best options. Now, with AT&T loosening
their reins on Android and 4G LTE phones coming out later this year,
hopefully a device will become available that I could safely recommend as an
excellent development device from AT&T. We'll just have to wait and see...

If you must have a CDMA device, I'd recommend getting a Samsung Fascinate.
As far as I'm aware, it is the only CDMA Android device currently available
on Verizon's lineup that doesn't have any crazy lockdown stuff like
encrypted bootloaders and stuff. The upcoming Droid Charge by Samsung may
also be an acceptable alternative, but it hasn't been released yet. Until it
is, we can't verify whether or not the dev interfaces are the same as on the
Samsung Fascinate. If they are, that's great. Otherwise, blech.

On Sprint, the Nexus S 4G is an acceptable device, but Android on it has
been modified somewhat to accept WiMAX, so it doesn't always behave as
expected when it comes to non-cellular network connections requested by
apps. I'd recommend a Samsung Transform as well if you want something from
Sprint.
