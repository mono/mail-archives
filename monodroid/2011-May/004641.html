On May 1, 2011, at 10:59 AM, Steve Sharrock wrote:
> I'm new to MonoDroid. I have a "proof-of-concept" app I've developed and tested on my own device using VS2010. Now I need to deploy the app to a half-dozen (or so) devices owned by the client company that will be using the app. How can I allow my client to install the app from one of their web-sites, or one of mine.

First, you need to have a Release build, so that you have a single .apk that you can deploy.

Once you have a Release build, you can throw the .apk on a web server and download it from Browser. Browser will download (but not install) the .apk. To install, drag down the status bar, tap the .apk that was just downloaded, and click the Install button.

The related problem is this: what cell phone company do you use? Some companies (AT&T) prevent "side-loading" of applications (which is what downloading from a URL entails), and thus the above process won't work with devices locked to these carriers. The only mechanism I know of to install apps on such locked devices is via the Android Market.
