Miguel has stated pretty flatly that WPF is an unlikely development for
Mono. I think somebody else would really need to step-up for that or else
somebody would have to come with a good business case to convince Miguel's
team to make it a priority.

Given the resources such an effort would likely pull away from other things,
I kind of wish that it does not happen myself. That said, it would be nice
if WPF was not the standard example given in every Internet forum for
proving that Mono is either not useful and/or hopelessly behind .NET in
terms of currency and maturity.

I am seeing less and less evidence that Mono is in trouble. Miguel's
messages have implied that a bit more patience and a bit less hysteria are
in order. Members of the Novell Mono team have continued to post commits to
Git and responses to the mailing lists. The tone of all of these imply to me
that they do not expect the projects to suddenly come to a halt. Not one
Novell employee has broken from the ranks to raise the alarm on the
"anonymous" Internet.

The CEO statement that "all roadmaps remain in place" does not fill me with
much comfort as sadly the public Mono roadmaps are very thin, lack
timelines, and are almost useless. For example, an exchange on the Mono
general mailing list recently downgraded the WCF support from "complete 4.0
API" to "the most useful parts of the Silverlight API". This only happened
when somebody asked about it. I do not think that the Mono team pays much
attention to these roadmaps. However, I presume that they have more robust
expectations internally and that these expectations are officially
unchanged.

I am cynical about the reliability of what CEOs say in general, especially
those I do not know the history of. However, Miguel and the Mono team have
always been pretty transparent and frank. This situation feels a lot like
when Apple threatened to bar MonoTouch and other third-party tools from the
App Store.

For now, I say "don't panic". We can figure out what is next for Mono when
the time comes, if it turns out we need to worry about it at all.
