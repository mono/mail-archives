On Apr 28, 2013, at 11:43 AM, Francesco Colombo <francesco.colombo at gmail.com> wrote:
> What I meant was if there were any other way to read assets from the Assets folder bypassing the  AssetsManager.

That should be possible. Assets are stored in the `.apk`, so you just need to bundle a .zip parser in your app (e.g. Ionic.Zip), grab the path to your `.apk`, and have at it:

	string apiFile = context.ApplicationInfo.SourceDir;
