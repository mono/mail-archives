On Tue, Nov 9, 2010 at 9:31 PM, Kevin Darty <kdarty at gmail.com> wrote:

> I think one important thing for all MonoDroid Developers to consider is
> whether or not their apps will be installable to SD.
>
> While many newer Android Devices have more internal storage, most of the
> old ones do not. I for one have a Nexus One with precious little Internal
> Storage so that remains high on my list of things to think about when
> installing Apps and typically I find myself removing large apps that only
> install to Internal Storage to make room for others.
>
> There are guidelines for what can and what can not be installed to SD so
> keep that in mind when writing your apps.
>
> You can review those guidelines here:
>
> http://goo.gl/2j99s
>
> You may need to think about this in terms of your development language
> choice or just how you deploy your app. If your application will require
> 10MB plus to install and can only install on Internal Storage you are going
> to get a ton of complaints in the Market and will likely miss out on sales.
>
> A good tip though is to provide addon Apps for those features that should
> NOT be installed to SD such as Widgets.
>
> I see a lot of really great Apps that can not be moved to SD simply because
> they provide Widgets and you are not "supposed" to move Apps with Widgets to
> SD because they will be unavailable if the SD Card is removed or mounted for
> USB access. Case in point, the official Twitter App is over 8MB and can't be
> moved to SD due to its Widgets. I personally have held my trigger finger
> over the install for that app several times as my Internal Storage spaces is
> precious.
>
> In cases like this, if your app will indeed be large but makes use of
> Widgets or any feature not recommended for movement to SD I would highly
> recommend breaking up your app into addons so that the main app can be moved
> to SD and then provide addon apps that are much lighter for Internal
> Storage.
>
> Just something to think about.
>
> -Kevin

>> You will be given an option between using the shared runtime (so the user
>> is directed on first launch to get the shared Mono runtime) or a fully
>> embedded Mono runtime in your application with both linking and stripping
>> applied to the result.
>>
>> The compressed version for the embedded version (linking+stripping) should
>> be about 4 megs.   This is just from comparing the equivalent sizes with
>> MonoTouch.
>>
>> You will never get to something like 24k as Android already ships the
>> JavaVM, but does no ship the Mono VM.   You do have to pay the price for
>> using the MonoVM in your app.
>>
>> For simple apps like a simple ToDo app, I am not sure there is much value
>> in using Mono.
