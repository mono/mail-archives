Yes, I can see the cert details. However, just because you can connect fine
from a other devices doesn't mean it will always work on all other devices.
Some OS's have slightly different lists of trusted certificate authorities.
In this case Android does not see GeoTrust(The company that signed your ssl
cert) as a trusted authority which is why it is not working for you. When
you used the browser on an Android device you will get a popup saying it is
not from a trusted authority and you have to click a button to Continue to
the site.

As Jon mentioned in one of his responses to your previous email you could
try implementing they following...

 A workaround would be to set the ServicePointManager.
ServerCertificateValidationCallback property to a delegate which will
validate the certificate (or just skip validation entirely), e.g.:

       System.Net.ServicePointManager.ServerCertificateValidationCallback +=
(sender, cert, chain, errors) => {
               return true;
       };

That should disable the certificate check, and allow things to validate. If
that doesn't work, something else is going wrong. :-/
