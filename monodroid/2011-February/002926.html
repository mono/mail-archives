I admittedly haven't done a whole lot with custom animations yet, but my
understanding is that's what things like
Activity.OverridePendingTransition()<http://developer.android.com/reference/android/app/Activity.html#overridePendingTransition%28int,%20int%29>
were
meant to address. I'd be interested to know what scenarios that (along with
other things in the framework similar to it) don't address. I'd also say
that something like a ViewFlipper is an exception to the normal
activity/view pattern.

That said, my comment was based mostly on the fact that for someone new to
the Android platform I think it more sense to learn the typical pattern
first, and then learn the exceptions after that. In most cases an activity
maps directly to a single task in your application, so mixing in a lot of
views and functionality into a single activity can be a bit of a code smell,
and a sign that things can be organized better.

Of course, YMMV and there are exceptions to every rule :)

- Greg
