Alisdair,

  Basically a browser port consists of 2 things (today):

#1> Functions to access the DOM
#2> Functions to download things with specific header controls thru the
browsers network stack (used for MMS).

Your browser must also support NPAPI.  If you take a look in the
plugin/firefox directory you can see our ff2 and ff3 bridges, and how
they're implemented.

Its also worth noting that pretty much everything from moonlight 1.0
works without these features today except for mms:// streams.
