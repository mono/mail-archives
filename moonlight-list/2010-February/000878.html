The following shows the values I get from the Silverlight key event handlers
on Mac OS X:

KeyPressed     KeyEventArgs.Key       KeyEventArgs.PlatformKeyCode

`              Key.Unknown (0xFF)     0x32
-              Key.Unknown (0xFF)     0x1B
=              Key.Unknown (0xFF)     0x18
[              Key.Unknown (0xFF)     0x21
]              Key.Unknown (0xFF)     0x1E
\              Key.Unknown (0xFF)     0x2A
;              Key.Unknown (0xFF)     0x29
'              Key.Unknown (0xFF)     0x27
,              Key.Unknown (0xFF)     0x2B
.              Key.Unknown (0xFF)     0x2F
/              Key.Unknown (0xFF)     0x2C
Shift ` (~)    Key.Unknown (0xFF)     0x32
Shift - (_)    Key.Unknown (0xFF)     0x1B
Shift = (+)    Key.Unknown (0xFF)     0x18
Shift [ ({)    Key.Unknown (0xFF)     0x21
Shift ] (})    Key.Unknown (0xFF)     0x1E
Shift \ (|)    Key.Unknown (0xFF)     0x2A
Shift ; (:)    Key.Unknown (0xFF)     0x29
Shift ' (")    Key.Unknown (0xFF)     0x27
Shift , (<)    Key.Unknown (0xFF)     0x2B
Shift . (>)    Key.Unknown (0xFF)     0x2F
Shift / (?)    Key.Unknown (0xFF)     0x2C

Like on Windows, the behaviour is consistent. The KeyEventArgs.Key is always
Key.Unknown and the KeyEventArgs.PlatformKeyCode is always the same given
the same KeyPressed (although different from on Windows) - regardless of
whether or not Shift is being pressed.
