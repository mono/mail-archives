On Thu, 2010-04-29 at 14:53 -0400, Brad Taylor wrote:
> Hey,
> 
> This awesome patch enhances the accessibility hooks added about a year
> ago to fully support Firefox's new native accessibility support[1]
> available in the next release of Firefox.
> 
> In particular, it allows Firefox and other NPAPI compliant browsers to
> obtain the root accessible for each plugin instance, and embed that
> object across process boundaries into the browser process.  Most of the
> heavy lifting for this feature exists in at-spi2, making the changes
> required to Moonlight minimal.  It does require an additional Moonlight
> Accessibility extension, but the feature gracefully degrades if the
> extension is not installed.
> 
> To see it in action, you need to be running at-spi2 trunk, the correct
> Firefox and a trunk version of our team's Moonlight Accessibility
> extension.  Right now, all are bleeding edge, but our team expects to
> have releases shortly that are consumable on openSUSE 11.2, Fedora 12
> and Ubuntu.
> 
> Thoughts?  Is this good to commit?
