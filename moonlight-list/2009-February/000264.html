After some talk with TuningMaster JB Evain we came to the conclusion
that it would be simpler to move the tuning process into moon itself.

That will:

* allow everyone to avoid updating both mono and mcs when changes are
made to the security attributes data files (that affects assemblies
presently tuned inside mcs). This is likely to occur quite a bit in the
early stages (and, in general, I dislike such "required" updates since
they are quite time consuming)

* make my life simpler to keep (and update) all tools/process/data in a
single place. That should make it less error-prone too so everyone will
benefit from this ;-)

Or in two words: "less pain" (except, temporarily, for JB ;-)


In technical terms it means that:

* /mcs has less needs to know about /moon (a good thing IMO)

* /moon will need to know a bit more about /mcs, i.e. its location to
get the 2.1 assemblies (defaulting to ../mcs from moon)

* tuning of the 2.1 assemblies from mcs will occurs when
	* they are updated inside /mcs (like today); or
	* when the security attributes data files are updated

* some of my earlier commits will move to /moon along with some of the
tuner stuff and makefile adjustments
