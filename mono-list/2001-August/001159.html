This night I couldn´t sleep well, after reading 
http://www.zdnet.com/enterprise/stories/linux/0,12249,2801560,00.html
"Hidden Pitfalls in .Net Open Source?" by Charles Babcock.

The problem I see is that we don´t have economic resources to keep searching 
all Microsoft patents, so it´s quite possible to inadvertently implement 
something that MS has patented, and have we trapped.

Personally, I have no money to pay attorneys or royalties, and even as we 
are not guilt of any wrong-doing a judge can order us to indemnify 
Microsoft, because after publishing something as GPL that conflicts with a 
patent, we can´t offer to get it back, ceasing to affect the patent´s owner 
licensing rights.

If Microsoft wants to play fair, it could publish, to everyone to see, a 
list of .NET related patents, so that we can keep ourselves out of trouble 
even if being less-compatible with the CLR/C#.
