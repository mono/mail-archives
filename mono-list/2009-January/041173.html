Hello all,

The talk schedule has gone up for for PyCon 2009 in Chicago. There are three
talks on IronPython, plus a tutorial on "Application Development with
IronPython on .NET or Mono" on the Wednesday before the conference.

The conference dates are:

   * March 25-26th Tutorial Days
   * March 27-29th Conference
   * March 30th - April 2nd Development Sprints

Jim Hugunin is one of the invited speakers, so his talk doesn't yet appear
on the schedule - but his usual talk is "IronPython the Road Ahead".

I've blogged about all the talks, plus the tutorial that Jonathan Hartley
and I will be giving, on IronPython URLs.

http://ironpython-urls.blogspot.com/2009/01/ironpython-at-pycon-2009.html

One of the talks is an introduction to FePy (the community distribution of
IronPython included in Mono): "Come see FePy (IronPython community edition)
in action via Mono -- a cross-platform open source implementation of the
.NET framework."

All the best,
