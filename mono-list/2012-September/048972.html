Microsoft, as well as developers around the world, are contributing to
C#/.NET to make better programming language to accelerate our
development. Newer versions of C#/.NET have more advanced features and
it evolutes gradually. If we stop following C#'s step, we are
seperating our C# from Microsoft's and on the contrary we are making
mono platform dependent (not compatible with C# on Windows). That's
not what we want.
