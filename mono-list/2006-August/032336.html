Hello,

    Both Phalanger, IronPython and Ruby.NET are interesting both from a
language perspective (they are one of the reasons we started to
implement Mono in the first place) and also because they exercise our
2.0 support.

    Since our 2.0 support is not officially complete, if you want to run
these things you need to use our trunk release of Mono.   In a way, they
are helpful in identifying what are the most important 2.0 features that
we need.
