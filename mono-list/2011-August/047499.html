Running with ./ means that the kernel actually knows it needs to do 'mono
./app' for executable files that are .Net programs. The fact that on your 32bit
machine this doesn't work just means that it's binfmt-misc setup isnt
configured for mono.
 
> Can I perhaps compile the app on Windows with Mono and not get this issue?

That shoudln't be how it works,  mono is quite capable of running a .net built
program.

The version of mono on your 32bit machine might be making the most difference 
in terms of performance.   You also mention queries, is this talking to a
database?
