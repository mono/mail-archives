No, you must pay for Visual Studio and "activate" it.  However, I have
been developing my application for some time now with SharpDevelop
(http://www.icsharpcode.net <http://www.icsharpcode.net/> ) and for the
most part it is pretty stable apart from a few minor quirks.
 
My local Barnes & Noble bookstore had some of the Microsoft Press .NET
books on sale at 40% off late last year and I picked up a few, each came
with a 60 day trial version of Visual Studio.NET Professional Edition.
My first trial edition expired.  Haven't bothered trying to install one
from another book yet.  It might not let you.
