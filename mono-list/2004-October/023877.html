If you contribute code to Mono, you know the license it is under, if=20
you have the 'GPL only' virus ^H^H^H^H^H religion ^H^H^H^H^H^H^H^H^H=20
disease ^H^H^H^H^H^H^H issue, then don't contribute your changes to the=20=

non GPL'ed portions of the code.  Otherwise contribute in good faith in=20=

the interests of promoting the community.  The core elements are dual=20
licensed, but a commercial / proprietary license is frequently required=20=

by vendors that might like to build upon the Mono code and Novell would=20=

be foolish to not bend to that where it is applicable.

Bear in mind these are the words of a developer that tries to=20
contribute, not those of anyone on the Mono core team and I am in no=20
way shape or form affiliated with Ximian or Novell, and this opinion is=20=

solely my own.

Andy
