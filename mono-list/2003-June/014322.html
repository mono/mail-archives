I really think Ximian should be picking up the tab for this ;-). There

Ximian site for other reasons. Someday, I think we should provide a
script that will build Mono from scratch on a reasonably well set up
computer (sort of like go.ximian.com), and I think that having
monocharge-mini on the official site would be a big part of this.
Anyways, Ximian probably has bigger pipes than got-mono ;-).

Another idea is to just keep the most modern monocharge-mini on CVS. I
actually *really* like this solution for a few reasons:
     1. Lets say someone breaks stuff one day after a new corlib
        compatibility thing. If I cvs update -D"48 hours ago", I will
        get the *correct* monocharge.
     2. The normal parties pick up the tab for bandwidth.

Miguel? What do you think of this?
