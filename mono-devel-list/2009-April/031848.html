Hi,

Our company N-iX (http://www.n-ix.com) was doing Mono porting to the MIPS
64-bit for the SiCortex, Inc. (http://sicortex.com).
We have finally merged with the official Mono SVN and prepared patches.
Before providing patches I would like to list the developers that made this
come true:
Kostyantyn Gushtin, Yevgen Kiruha, Serhiy Naumenko, Serhiy Stetskovych and
Ian Dichkovsky.

We have put 10 patches to the bugzilla.
Bug 497320 - [PATCH] Set of Mono MIPS64-bit port patches
https://bugzilla.novell.com/show_bug.cgi?id=497320

Our primary goal was porting to the 64-bit platform. Unfortunatelly, we
haven't had a chance to test our changes on the box with o32 ABI. There is a
probability that we broke something. At least as far as we know, the 64-bit
port is the most usable among MIPS ports to this day. We this port we have
got quite good results, since regression, extensive and major part of Class
Library tests (99,60%) are passed successfully.

Please, review the patches and check them in. We are open for their
improving.
There is a one more good news. Andrew Jorgensen has set up the monobuild
system on our development SiCortex machine, so everybody could track daily
build and test results of your reviewing efforts here:
http://mono.ximian.com/monobuild/python/monobuild.py/packagestatus?platform=gentoo-1-mips&package=mono&HEAD_or_RELEASE=HEAD

Regards,
Ian
