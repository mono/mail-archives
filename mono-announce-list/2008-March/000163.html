Hi Everyone,

This email is to announce Tomboy 0.10.0.  This is the beginning of the
stable 0.10.x series and will coincide with GNOME 2.22.x.

Tomboy has been branched as "gnome-2-22" for stable (0.10.x)
development.  SVN trunk is now open for new feature development
(0.11.x).


What is Tomboy
=============

Tomboy is a simple personal note-taking application designed to be
unobtrusive and friendly, while supporting inter-note-links similar to
a WikiWikiWeb to help you organize your notes and ideas.  It can run
either as a GNOME panel applet or a notification tray icon.

Tomboy's Website: http://www.gnome.org/projects/tomboy/

Tomboy's Wiki: http://live.gnome.org/Tomboy
 * Road Map: http://live.gnome.org/Tomboy/RoadMap
 * Brainstorming: http://live.gnome.org/Tomboy/PlaceForNewIdeas

What's new in Tomboy 0.10.0
=======================

* Organize your notes into Notebooks
* New Insert Timestamp Addin
* HTML Export formatting improvements
* Bulleted-list handling improvements
* Drag and drop links (web/file/etc.) improvements
* Added note searching and closing via DBus
* Remove bundled ndesk-dbus-* packages (they're in GNOME now)
* Tasks Addin removed [1]
* Many bugs resolved (see below)

Changes since 0.9.8 (the last development release) include the following:
* Updated documentation to cover new features in 0.10.0 (#520513, Paul Cutler).
* Translation updates: da, de, el, en_GB, es, eu, he, hi, hu, oc, pl,
pt_BR, ru, ta, uk, zh_HK, zh_TW

The next six months...
=================

Looking forward to 0.12.0, some of the following ideas have popped
up: 1) Making Tomboy more cross-platform compatible (run on Mac
and Windows using Gtk#), 2) Adding attachments to notes so you
can drag and drop pictures/documents/etc. into a note, 3) [suggest
something yourself]...

Of course, since many of the authors of Tomboy work on it "in their
spare time" anything's possible.  You can help!  Please share your
ideas with us on the Tomboy Mailing List [2], by submitting a feature
in GNOME's Bugzilla [3], or by adding them to our Wiki [4].  We'd
love to see new contributors join us; submit a one-line patch or
write an entirely new feature.
